Once I have a few very rough conceptual ideas, maybe two or three distinct directions, that's when I'd consider who to consult. I wouldn't go to anyone empty-handed; that feels unproductive. After a discussion with Takahashi-san, where we might refine one or two of my initial concepts or even spark a new one, I might consider talking to Tanaka-san next. Her intuitive approach could be a fantastic counterpoint to my (and Takahashi-san's) more reflective style. She might look at a concept and immediately see a bold, unconventional gameplay idea or a catchy slogan that I wouldn't have considered. She could inject that creative spark and broaden the appeal.

I'm not ruling out Nakamura-san from the Sports Game Planning department either. While he's in a completely different genre, an intuitive mind like his could offer an entirely fresh perspective that's unburdened by post-apocalyptic conventions. Maybe he'd see a competitive element or a unique movement mechanic that could surprisingly fit into a "post-apocalyptic" world in a way we hadn't imagined. But that would probably be a bit later, once I have something more solid to share, and maybe after getting some internal departmental feedback.

So, in short:
1.  **Phase 1 (Alone):** Deep dive into brainstorming various post-apocalyptic scenarios, world-building, and core gameplay hooks. Develop a few distinct, rough concepts.
2.  **Phase 2 (Consult first):** Approach Takahashi-san. We share a similar work style, and I respect her depth of thought. She could help me refine the core story, concept, and thematic elements.
