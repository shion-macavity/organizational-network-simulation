The core concept is that players don't just control one character; they control an *ancestral line*. You start as a character in, say, a modern or near-future setting, live out their life, make choices, build relationships, develop skills, and then, at some point, that character passes on. Then, you continue playing as their child, or even a grandchild, inheriting aspects of the previous generation's life – their physical traits, learned skills, resources, and most importantly, the *consequences* of their decisions.

**Story:** The overarching story isn't about saving the world from an alien invasion. It's about building a multi-generational legacy in a dynamic, evolving world. Perhaps there's a slow-burn mystery woven through the generations about a family secret, a historical event, or even a subtle environmental shift that becomes more apparent over time. Each generation faces unique challenges and opportunities based on the world state left by their predecessors. For example, if a previous generation heavily industrialized a region, a later generation might face pollution issues and resource scarcity. Conversely, if an ancestor invested in community projects or sustainable practices, their descendants might enjoy a more prosperous and harmonious environment.

**Contents:**
*   **Deep Life Simulation:** Players manage daily life, careers, education, relationships, hobbies, and health. Choices during character creation and throughout life determine their personality traits and skill progression.
*   **Generational Progression:** When a character dies (either naturally or through various in-game events), the player selects their successor from their children or adopted heirs. The new character inherits a "legacy score" and a modified skill tree based on the previous character's achievements and choices.
*   **Dynamic World State:** The game world is constantly changing based on player actions and simulated events. Cities grow or decline, ecosystems shift, political landscapes evolve. These changes carry across generations, making the impact of choices tangible.
*   **Personal and Societal Impact:** Decisions aren't just personal. Developing a certain technology might lead to a new industry but also social unrest. Advocating for a specific cause might change laws for future generations.
*   **Mystery/Discovery Elements:** Hidden lore, family secrets, and environmental mysteries that can only be fully uncovered by piecing together clues across multiple generations. Maybe there are specific heirlooms that carry clues, or locations that reveal new information over time.
*   **Relationship System:** Very robust, with friendships, rivalries, romantic relationships, and family dynamics influencing character development and future generations. Who you marry significantly impacts your offspring's potential.

**Slogan:** "Live Your Legacy. Shape Their Future." Or maybe, "Your Choices Echo Through Generations." I like the second one a bit more. "Your Choices Echo Through Generations." Yeah, that feels right.

**Key Selling Points:**
1.  **Unprecedented Generational Storytelling:** Experience a unique narrative where your actions have profound, lasting consequences across multiple playable generations.
2.  **Dynamic, Evolving World:** Witness the direct impact of your legacy as the game world transforms and responds to your family's decisions over decades, even centuries, of gameplay.
3.  **Deep Personal Connection:** Develop intimate bonds with your characters and their families, feeling the weight of their lives and the hope for their descendants.
4.  **Infinite Replayability:** Every playthrough creates a unique family saga with branching paths, different world states, and new mysteries to uncover.
