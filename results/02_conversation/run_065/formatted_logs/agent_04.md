My first thought for a game concept... I've always liked the idea of a crime story with a unique mechanic. What if it's not just *solving* a crime, but *reliving* it? Like, a "Groundhog Day" type of scenario, but with a dark, gritty crime thriller twist.

Okay, let's try to flesh that out a bit.
**Concept:** A time-loop crime investigation game. You're stuck in a fixed period—say, 24 hours—leading up to a major, devastating crime. Every time the loop resets, you retain the knowledge you gained, but you have to live through that same day again, trying to change events, prevent the crime, or at least uncover the full truth and catch the perpetrator.
**Story:** You could play as a rookie detective, or even just an ordinary person who gets caught up in a city-wide catastrophe, like a terrorist bombing or a high-profile serial killer's final act. You witness the event, try to intervene, perhaps you even die, and then you wake up at the start of that very same day. It’s a race against time, but with the unique advantage of infinite attempts to piece together clues, understand motives, and change destinies.
**Contents/Gameplay:**
*   **Investigation and Deduction:** Each loop, you explore different locations, interrogate different people, collect new pieces of evidence. The map would evolve as you uncover secrets or influence characters. Maybe there's a "mind map" or "deduction board" where you link clues across loops.
*   **Choice and Consequence:** Your actions in one loop could have subtle or dramatic impacts on subsequent loops. Saving someone in one loop might give you a crucial piece of information later, or perhaps even put you in more danger.
*   **Action/Stealth elements:** It wouldn't just be talking. There could be moments where you need to discreetly follow a suspect, infiltrate a location, or even engage in a quick action sequence to stop something from happening.
*   **Puzzle Solving:** Environmental puzzles, code-breaking, or even social engineering puzzles to get information out of reluctant NPCs.
*   **Multiple Paths/Endings:** Success would be preventing the crime entirely and bringing the culprit to justice. But there could be partial successes, like identifying the culprit but failing to stop the crime, or even tragic failures where the loop continues but the situation worsens.
**Slogan:** "Relive the Day. Rewrite the Crime." Or maybe something a bit more impactful... "The Loop is Your Witness. The Future is Your Choice." I think I like the second one better, it sounds a bit more proactive.
**Key Selling Points:**
1.  **Unique Time-Loop Mechanic:** A fresh take on the crime genre, offering replayability and a deep sense of discovery.
2.  **Player-Driven Narrative:** Every choice matters, leading to diverse outcomes and a truly personalized story.
3.  **Intense Deduction and Thriller Atmosphere:** Players aren't just solving a puzzle; they're actively trying to *prevent* a disaster, building tension with each loop.

Okay, that's a good starting point for my own thoughts. Now, who should I talk to about this?

Yamamoto-san, our leader, will be the one summarizing everything, so I'll definitely need to share my ideas with him eventually. He's balanced, so he'd probably appreciate a well-thought-out concept.

Suzuki-san is reflective, he's got a lot of experience. He might poke holes in my idea, but in a constructive way, helping me refine it. He could probably see potential complications I wouldn't think of.
Tanaka-san and Ito-san are both intuitive. They might get excited about the core concept quickly, or have immediate, creative additions. Tanaka-san might have some great story beats, while Ito-san might see how to make the gameplay really engaging.

I think I'll start by talking to Suzuki-san first. He's balanced like Yamamoto-san but generally has more time for in-depth discussion, and his reflective nature means he'll likely consider my idea thoroughly before offering feedback. His experience could help me solidify the concept and prepare it for Yamamoto-san. Plus, getting a more senior general member's input first might make my pitch to the leader stronger.
