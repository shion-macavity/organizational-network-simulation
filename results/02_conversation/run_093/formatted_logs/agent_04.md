*   **Gameplay Loop:** Travel between diverse biomes and communities (desert nomads, forest dwellers, technologically advanced ruins, agrarian settlements). Each community has unique needs, resources, and cultural biases.
*   **Core Mechanics:**
    *   **Resource Management:** Collect unique regional resources, craft essential goods (tools, medicine, food, art), and manage supply chains. Resources aren't just for building, but also for cultural exchange – a rare dye might be traded for knowledge, or a unique instrument for peace treaties.
    *   **Diplomacy & Persuasion:** Engage in dialogue with community leaders, negotiate treaties, resolve disputes between factions, and learn their histories.     *   **Infrastructure Building:** Help communities build essential structures – irrigation systems, clean water sources, communication networks, schools, cultural centers. These improve happiness, productivity, and interconnectivity.
    *   **Narrative Events:** Dynamic events based on player choices and the state of the world – natural disasters, bandit attacks (which you resolve diplomatically or by building defenses, not direct combat), cultural festivals, or emergent faction conflicts.
    *   **Character Development:** The Nomad Architect gains skills in diplomacy, engineering, cultural understanding, and resourcefulness. Perhaps companion characters with their own specializations join you.

The **slogan**? How about "Build Bridges, Not Walls. Weave Peace, Not War." Or maybe something punchier... "Echoes of Yesterday. Architects of Tomorrow." Yeah, I like that second one better. "Echoes of Yesterday. Architects of Tomorrow." It hints at the past war but focuses on the future.

And **key selling points**?
1.  **Unique "Post-Conflict" Genre:** Focuses on rebuilding and diplomacy rather than direct combat.
2.  **Rich Narrative & Choice-Driven Diplomacy:** Every decision matters, shaping the fate of diverse communities and the entire world.
3.  **Engaging Resource & Infrastructure Management:** Satisfying loops of gathering, crafting, and constructing to see tangible progress.
4.  **Explore a Beautiful, Broken World:** A compelling setting with varied biomes and unique cultures to discover.

This isn't strictly military/war, but it takes the *strategic thinking* and *impact of conflict* and flips it to the positive side. It's got that "big picture" feel that I think our department can appreciate, even if it's not about tanks and battles.
