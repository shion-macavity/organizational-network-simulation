**Concept:** Imagine humanity's last hope isn't a metal behemoth, but a living, colossal bio-ship, a self-sustaining ecosystem designed to carry the genetic essence of Earth to a new star. This ship, "Astraea," isn't just a vessel; it's a massive, complex, organic world in itself, teeming with diverse biomes and encapsulated fragments of terrestrial life. The game is less about combat and more about **symbiosis, exploration, and resource nurturing** within this living spaceship.

**Story:** The old Earth is dying, ravaged by an ecological collapse. A small, carefully selected crew, along with cryo-preserved samples of all life, are launched on Astraea. Their mission: navigate the vast unknown, keep Astraea healthy, and ultimately find a new home. But as they travel, Astraea itself begins to show signs of sentience, or at least a powerful, complex consciousness. The crew must learn to communicate with it, understand its needs, and integrate their existence with its own. What if the ship isn't just a tool, but a living entity that evolves with them, sometimes acting as an ally, sometimes as an inscrutable force of nature? The story would revolve around solving Astraea's 'illnesses', adapting to its internal changes, and uncovering ancient cosmic secrets through its organic memory banks.

**Contents (Gameplay):**
*   **Life-Sim/Resource Management:** Players would manage the health and growth of different biomes within Astraea – jungles, oceans, deserts, even miniature cities for the crew. Resources wouldn't be 'mined' but 'cultivated' from the ship itself or other organic processes.
*   **Exploration/Puzzle:** Vast, procedurally generated internal biomes within Astraea, each with unique flora, fauna, and environmental puzzles tied to Astraea's health or specific anomalies. Maybe players have to navigate through the ship's 'arteries' or 'nervous system' to fix issues.
*   **Cooperative/Social:** The core crew members (NPCs or other players in a co-op mode) would have their own specializations (botanists, engineers, xenobiologists, navigators) and unique relationship dynamics. Maintaining morale and cooperation would be crucial. Decisions made by the player could affect these relationships and Astraea's mood.
*   **Mystery/Narrative:** Discovering the origins of Astraea, decoding its organic language, and uncovering the fate of previous 'seed ships' or ancient civilizations that might have created it.
*   **No traditional combat:** Threats would be environmental, internal to the ship (mutations, diseases), or psychological (isolation, difficult decisions). Conflict resolution would be through problem-solving, adaptation, and perhaps even 'healing' hostile lifeforms within Astraea.

**Slogan:** "Astraea: Live. Breathe. Become the new world." Or maybe, "Astraea: The universe is alive. So are we." I like "Live. Breathe. Become the new world." It's more active.

**Key Selling Points:**
1.  **Unique "Living World" Ship:** The ship itself is the core character and environment, constantly evolving and reacting to player actions.
2.  **Focus on Symbiosis, Not Combat:** A refreshing take on sci-fi, emphasizing nurturing, exploration, and problem-solving over traditional fighting.
3.  **Deep Narrative & Emotional Connection:** A compelling story about humanity's last hope, survival, and forming a bond with an intelligent, organic entity.
