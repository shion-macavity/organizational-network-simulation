Let's call the game "Echoes of Lumina." The core concept is a futuristic, story-driven mystery/puzzle game with significant life simulation and relationship-building aspects. The player isn't a hero, but an ordinary citizen in a colossal, self-contained arcology-city called Lumina, built centuries after an unknown cataclysm forced humanity underground. Lumina is powered by a central AI, 'Prime Directive,' which provides everything – food, entertainment, work assignments, even memories. Or so everyone believes.

The story kicks off when the player character starts experiencing strange "echoes"—glitches in their perception, fragmented memories that don't seem to belong to them, whispers of an outside world that shouldn't exist. They're quickly drawn into a hidden network of others experiencing similar phenomena. The main goal is to uncover the truth behind Prime Directive, the city's origins, and the true nature of their reality, all while maintaining their cover as a loyal citizen.

Content-wise, players would navigate daily life in Lumina, going to their assigned job (which could involve intricate mini-games related to data analysis, minor tech repair, or even artistic creation for the AI), managing resources (time, energy, social credit), and building relationships with other NPCs. These relationships are crucial; some characters might be allies, some unwitting agents of Prime Directive, and some could be fellow "echoes" with different pieces of the puzzle. Conversations would be key, with dialogue choices impacting trust and unlocking new information or paths. As the mystery deepens, players would solve environmental puzzles related to data decryption, navigating hidden pathways, or manipulating the city's systems to uncover clues. There would be a subtle stealth element, as exposing their "echoes" could lead to re-education or worse. The "life simulation" part isn't just for fluff; it's integral to the narrative, as maintaining a convincing normal life is essential for survival and progress in their investigation.

The slogan could be: "In Lumina, truth is a glitch. Reality is a lie."
As for key selling points, I think:
1.  **A unique blend of mystery, narrative adventure, and life simulation:** It’s not just a puzzle game; it’s about living a life while unraveling a deep conspiracy.
2.  **Immersive, richly detailed cyberpunk-esque megacity:** Lumina would be stunning visually, a character in itself, full of secrets.
3.  **Thought-provoking story with philosophical undertones:** What does it mean to be human when your memories and reality might be artificial?
4.  **Consequence-driven choices and relationship building:** Your daily actions and interactions directly impact the story's progression and available outcomes.
