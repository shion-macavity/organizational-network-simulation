How about something that takes the strategic depth of a military game but applies it to a completely different, unexpected setting? Not actual war, but a 'conflict' or 'challenge' of a different nature. For instance, what if we made a game about resource management and territorial expansion, but in a post-apocalyptic world where nature has completely reclaimed cities, and players control groups of survivalists trying to rebuild society? The "military" aspect could come in through managing squads for expeditions, defending against aggressive wildlife or other factions, and perhaps even engineering defensive structures, but the core isn't about traditional warfare with tanks and soldiers. It's about survival, exploration, and community building.

Let's call the concept: "Echoes of Eden." The story could be that humanity barely survived a global ecological collapse. Centuries later, small, isolated communities exist, clinging to life. Players lead one such community, starting from a small encampment in the ruins of a forgotten city, slowly expanding their territory, scavenging for ancient tech and resources, and rediscovering lost knowledge.

The content could involve:
1.  **Exploration:** Send out scouting parties into overgrown, procedurally generated cityscapes, finding ancient tech, seeds, building materials. This could have elements of an RPG, with customizable characters gaining skills.
2.  **Base Building:** A deep life simulation and resource management system where players build and upgrade their settlement, growing food, purifying water, crafting tools, and constructing defensive walls.
3.  **Diplomacy/Conflict:** Encounter other factions – some peaceful, some hostile. Players can choose to trade, form alliances, or engage in tactical squad-based combat using salvaged weapons, perhaps even tamed mutant animals. This is where the "military" element could subtly come in, but it's about survival and protecting your community, not conquest.
4.  **Environmental Challenges:** Dynamic weather, mutated flora and fauna, dwindling resources would provide constant challenges.

The slogan could be: "Reclaim. Rebuild. Remember."
And the key selling points:
1.  Deep strategic base building combined with engaging squad-based exploration and tactical combat.
2.  A rich, immersive post-apocalyptic world where nature has reclaimed civilization.
3.  Meaningful choices affecting diplomacy and community development.
4.  A story of hope and resilience in the face of overwhelming odds.

Yeah, that sounds like a good starting point. It touches on resource management, tactical thinking, and a sense of growth, which our department has experience with, but wraps it in a fresh, non-traditional "war" context. It definitely allows for "outside the box" thinking in terms of art style and narrative.

So, how to approach this? I think I'll refine these ideas a bit more. Maybe draw up some rough visual inspirations in my head. Then, perhaps, I'll casually mention to Takahashi-san that I'm thinking about something combining survival with strategy, and see what her initial reaction or ideas are. Or even Tanaka-san, since he's balanced like me; he might have some practical insights on how to make a concept like this work without going too far afield from what we know. A quick chat over coffee, nothing too formal. Just to get another perspective before fleshing it out completely for Yamamoto-san. That way, I'm not just presenting *my* idea, but something that's had a little bit of preliminary 'testing' with colleagues. Yes, that seems like a balanced approach.
