**Story:**
A future Earth, ravaged by climate change and resource depletion, is on the verge of collapse. The last hope lies with Project ARK, sending a fleet of advanced starships carrying a diverse crew of scientists, engineers, medics, and security personnel – organized under the ERF – to Elysium. Their mission: establish a foothold, harvest critical resources, and terraform the planet to make it a new home for humanity.
Upon arrival, they find Elysium breathtakingly beautiful, with unique flora and fauna. But as they begin to establish their first base, the planet begins to fight back. Indigenous life forms, far more intelligent and interconnected than initially understood, perceive humanity as a destructive force. The ERF soon realizes they aren't just colonizers; they are invaders in a war they didn't know they started, against a planet that is literally alive and hostile. The story unfolds as the ERF tries to understand Elysium, not just conquer it, and make impossible choices between survival, ethics, and the ultimate fate of two worlds.

**Contents (Gameplay Elements):**
*   **Cooperative Base Building & Management:** Players work together to establish and expand a modular base, managing resources, power, defenses (against environmental threats, not just hostile fauna), and crew morale. Think detailed resource chains and infrastructure.
*   **Procedural Exploration:** Elysium is a vast, dynamically generated world with diverse biomes (dense bioluminescent jungles, volcanic plains, crystalline deserts, deep ocean trenches). Exploration is risky but necessary for new resources and understanding the planet.
*   **Adaptive Ecosystem AI:** The planet's flora and fauna aren't just static enemies. They react to player actions. Aggressive harvesting might provoke large-scale environmental responses. Over-reliance on certain areas could lead to ecological collapse, or new, more dangerous mutated lifeforms.
*   **Specialized Roles:** Players choose from different ERF specializations (e.g., Lead Engineer, Xenobotanist, Reconnaissance Specialist, Security Commander, Chief Medical Officer), each with unique skills, equipment, and research trees. This fosters teamwork and reliance on each other.
*   **Narrative Choices & Diplomacy:** Not all "war" is fought with guns. Players will encounter ancient, intelligent non-human entities and strange planetary phenomena that require diplomatic solutions, scientific understanding, or difficult moral choices that impact the planet's aggression levels and the story's branching paths.
*   **Research & Development:** Unlock new technologies for survival, adaptation, environmental manipulation, and understanding the planet. This could include advanced terraforming tools, non-lethal defense mechanisms, or bio-integrated suits.
*   **"War" against the elements:** Extreme weather, seismic activity, bio-hazards, and unique planetary defenses (e.g., giant plant defenses, atmospheric anomalies) are constant threats.
