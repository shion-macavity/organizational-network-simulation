Let's try to flesh out a core concept. How about a game set in a near-future, post-cataclysmic world, but not the typical gritty survival shooter. Instead, humanity has retreated into vast, self-sustaining underground "arks" or "citadels." The "military" isn't about fighting other humans anymore. It's about deploying highly specialized teams to the surface to combat the slow, inexorable decay of the environment, or the constant threat of new, mutated flora and fauna that actively dismantle the remaining human infrastructure.

Okay, so the **concept** could be: "Eco-Militia: The Great Reclamation." It's a strategic life-simulation/resource management game with action-RPG elements for the surface expeditions.

**Story idea:** After a global ecological collapse known as "The Great Withering," humanity lives in subterranean citadels, barely clinging to existence. The surface is toxic, overgrown, and dangerous. The last hope lies with the "Eco-Militia," special units tasked with venturing to the surface to find and cultivate specific resilient plant species, purify contaminated zones, construct automated environmental processors, and defend against mutated wildlife. Their ultimate goal is to slowly, painstakingly, make the surface habitable again for future generations. It's a multi-generational mission.

**Contents:**
1.  **Citadel Management (Life Sim/Resource Management):** Players manage their underground citadel, allocating resources (water, energy, processed surface materials) to research, training, and infrastructure. They oversee the growth of their population, keep morale up, and make tough decisions about resource allocation. This is where the 'military' organization comes in - a highly structured society dedicated to a common goal.
2.  **Surface Expeditions (Action-RPG/Tactical Combat):** Players assemble and control a small squad of Eco-Militia specialists (each with unique skills, gear, and maybe even a drone companion) for missions to the surface. Missions could involve:
    *   **Scouting & Mapping:** Uncovering new areas, identifying threats.
    *   **Resource Harvesting:** Collecting rare seeds, water samples, ancient tech.
    *   **Purification & Cultivation:** Deploying environmental purifiers, planting hardy vegetation.
    *   **Defense & Containment:** Setting up defensive perimeters, repelling mutated creatures, dealing with environmental hazards like toxic storms or aggressive plant growths.
3.  **Progression:** As players succeed, their citadel expands, new technologies are unlocked (better gear, advanced purification methods, automated surface drones), and the surface slowly shows signs of healing, offering new areas to explore and new challenges. There could be a legacy system where retired squad members contribute to research or training, and new recruits carry on their skills.
4.  **Dynamic World:** The surface environment isn't static. It constantly evolves, with new threats emerging, weather patterns changing, and areas either improving or deteriorating based on player actions.

**Slogan:** "Heal the Earth. Reclaim the Future. One Seed at a Time."
Or, perhaps: "The Last War: Fought for Life."
