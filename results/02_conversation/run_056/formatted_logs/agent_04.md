* **【初期思考】**
> Okay, let's get this brain-dump started. Military/War Game department, but the brief says "broad strategic direction" and "think outside the box." That's my kind of brief! I'm Watanabe, intuitive, so I'm just going to throw some wild ideas out there and see what sticks. No point in overthinking it right now.

Military games... usually means guns, battles, strategy, right? But what if the "war" isn't a traditional one? What if it's a different kind of conflict entirely? I mean, "war" can be against anything, really. Diseases, disasters, even yourself.

My mind immediately jumps to something like... what if it's not about *fighting* a war, but about *preventing* one? Or dealing with the *aftermath*? Or maybe the "military" isn't what we expect it to be.

Okay, here's a thought: What if the game takes place after a massive, devastating global conflict – maybe not even human vs. human, but something alien or environmental – and the remaining "military" forces are now tasked with rebuilding society and preserving humanity's legacy? It flips the genre on its head. Instead of destruction, it's about construction and preservation.

Let's call it... "Echoes of Eden" or "The Seed Keepers." Something that sounds hopeful, but with a hint of past struggle.

**Concept:** The world has been ravaged, but not by conventional war. Instead, a cosmic event or a devastating, silent ecological collapse has rendered most of the planet uninhabitable. Small pockets of humanity survive, protected by remnant military-turned-scientific-and-logistical units. Our goal isn't to fight enemies with guns, but to safeguard Earth's biodiversity, knowledge, and potential for future civilization. We're fighting against extinction, against forgetfulness, against hopelessness.

**Story:** Players lead one of these "Seed Keeper" units. They operate out of a mobile, fortified habitat – a massive, self-sustaining ark or a repurposed military super-carrier – traveling across a desolate, beautiful, yet dangerous world. Their mission is to locate, identify, and collect genetic material of flora and fauna, scan and archive forgotten human knowledge (books, art, historical data), and carefully cultivate new, sustainable ecosystems in designated "Eden Zones." There could be remnants of old-world technology to reactivate, ancient secrets to uncover about the collapse, and difficult choices about which species to prioritize saving when resources are critically low. Maybe there are small, scattered human settlements to aid, or even hostile pockets who believe in a different path to survival, adding a layer of ethical dilemma without resorting to direct combat.

**Gameplay Style:** This feels like it could be a blend of a few things.
*   **Life Simulation/Resource Management:** Managing the ark's resources, personnel, energy, and internal biomes.
*   **Exploration/Adventure:** Venturing out into the vast, procedural or semi-procedural world map in specialized vehicles to discover new areas, collect samples, and find ruins. This would have elements of environmental puzzle-solving and navigation challenges.
*   **Cooperative/Strategic:** Maybe even a cooperative multiplayer aspect, where different players lead different departments on the ark (e.g., one manages the agricultural biomes, another leads expeditions, a third focuses on research and development). Or it could be single-player with AI-managed departments.
*   **Narrative/Mystery:** Uncovering the truth of the collapse, making difficult ethical choices, and deciding humanity's future.

**Contents (Specifics):**
*   **The Ark/Habitat:** This is our central hub. Players upgrade it, manage its systems (water recycling, energy, air filtration, cultivation labs, archives). Different modules could be unlocked and customized.
*   **Expedition Vehicles:** A fleet of specialized vehicles for different terrains – all-terrain crawlers, atmospheric drones, submersible research vessels. Each with upgradeable tools for scanning, sampling, and environmental analysis.
*   **Biodiversity Index:** A core mechanic. Players collect data on species, add them to the ark's database, and try to breed or cultivate them in controlled environments. Each species has unique properties that can help restore an ecosystem.
*   **Knowledge Archive:** Collecting old-world data, repairing corrupted files, piecing together history. This could unlock technologies or provide crucial narrative clues.
*   **Eden Zones:** Specific areas on the world map where the player can begin to re-seed and rebuild ecosystems, creating thriving oases in the desolation. This would involve a long-term strategic element of planning what to plant where.
*   **Environmental Hazards:** Dynamic weather events, mutated flora/fauna (not necessarily hostile, but perhaps difficult to sample or navigate), geological instability. These are the "enemies" of the game.

**Slogan:** "Beyond the battlefield, humanity's greatest fight begins: to reclaim, to remember, to rebuild." Or, more punchy: "The World is Broken. We are the Seeds."

**Key Selling Points:**
1.  **A Unique Take on "War":** Reimagines the military genre as a struggle for survival, preservation, and rebirth, rather than combat.
2.  **Deep & Meaningful Gameplay:** Combines exploration, resource management, and strategic world-building with a strong narrative focus on humanity's legacy.
3.  **Hope in Desolation:** Offers a refreshingly optimistic yet challenging post-apocalyptic experience, focusing on creation rather than just survival.
4.  **Ethical Dilemmas:** Players face tough choices about what to save, how to allocate resources, and the very definition of "humanity's future."

This feels pretty good for an initial, intuitive blast. It's got that "outside the box" feel, and it still incorporates a "military" structure in its organized, mission
