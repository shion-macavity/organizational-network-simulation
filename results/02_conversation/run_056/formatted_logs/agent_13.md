My mind immediately jumps to something beyond just *living* a life. What if it's about *shaping* lives, but not in a god-game kind of way? More personal, more intimate. We're "Slice of Life," right? What's the *essence* of a slice of life, but exaggerated or recontextualized?

Maybe a game where you play as an object? No, too abstract. What about a non-human entity witnessing and gently influencing human lives across generations? A bit like a guardian spirit, or an old, wise tree in a village. That feels very "slice of life" but with a fantastical twist. It's about observation, subtle intervention, the slow passage of time, the beauty and tragedy of everyday existence.

Okay, let's run with that for a moment. Imagine a game where you embody something ancient and timeless, maybe a sentient river, or the spirit of a mountain, or even just an old, forgotten clock in a town square. Your objective isn't to *win* in a traditional sense, but to guide the emotional and social fabric of the people around you. You don't directly control them; you just… nudge things. Maybe you cause a rare flower to bloom at a pivotal moment, or your mountain spirit creates a gentle mist that clears someone's mind, or the river's current carries a lost letter to its destination.

The story would be generational. You'd see families grow, change, face challenges, celebrate joys, all from this unique, long-term perspective. The concept is "Subtle Impact, Eternal Witness."

Contents could include:
*   **Observation System:** Where you "focus" on different families or individuals to understand their dilemmas.
*   **Influence Mechanics:** A suite of gentle, nature-based or object-based "actions" that have ripple effects. These wouldn't be direct commands, but more like environmental changes or auspicious omens.
*   **Progression:** Not through levels, but through the cultural and emotional development of the community you oversee. Unlocking new "influence abilities" as the community evolves.
*   **Narrative Arcs:** Multiple branching stories determined by your subtle nudges, culminating in different "endings" for the community – prosperous, harmonious, fractured, etc.

Slogan? Hmm, something evocative... "Whispers of the Ages, Tides of Humanity." Or maybe, "The Unseen Hand, The Enduring Heart."

Key selling points:
1.  **Unique Perspective:** Play as an ancient, non-human entity witnessing and guiding human civilization.
2.  **Emotional Depth:** Focus on multi-generational stories, human connections, and the subtle beauty of everyday life.
3.  **Non-Violent & Thought-Provoking Gameplay:** Strategic influence through environmental interaction, rather than direct control or conflict.
4.  **Endless Replayability:** Different communities, varied influence choices, and unpredictable human interactions create unique narrative outcomes.

That's a pretty strong start for an intuitive brainstorm. It's got the "slice of life" core with a fantasy wrapper. It's not action, not RPG in the traditional sense, maybe more of a puzzle/strategy/life-sim hybrid?

Yamada is also intuitive, so he'd probably get the abstract appeal.

Then, once I have a bit more shape, I'd bring it to the whole department. Get Nakamura, Kobayashi, Kato, and Yoshida to help ground it a bit. As for Yamamoto in the other department... I could actually see this idea being something he might find interesting, precisely *because* it's so different from military/war games.
