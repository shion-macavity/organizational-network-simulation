It's a good team.

First thing's first, I need to get everyone together. My approach would be to kick off a really open-ended brainstorming session. I don't want to lead too much at the beginning, just set the stage. I'll tell them exactly what the company wants: story, concept, contents, slogan, key selling points, all based on *free ideas*. I'll explicitly state that the "Post-Apocalyptic" genre is just a broad strategic direction for our department, not a cage. They should feel completely free to mix in elements from any other genre – RPG, puzzle, even something as unexpected as a life sim or racing game within a post-apocalyptic setting. The more unusual, the better, at this stage.

I think the key is to not just ask for game *ideas*, but for *feelings* and *experiences* they want players to have. What kind of world do they envision? What kind of challenges? What kind of emotional journey? Do they want it to be desolate, or vibrant? Lonely, or communal? Focusing on the core experience first can help us build outwards.

So, who to consult first? Honestly, I'm going to consult *everyone* in the department at the same time for this initial brainstorm. I want all their raw, unfiltered thoughts hitting the table simultaneously. Sometimes, one person's slightly off-the-wall idea can spark something brilliant in another. I wouldn't want to limit that cross-pollination. I'll make sure to create an environment where no idea is a bad idea, especially at the start. We're just throwing paint at the canvas.

My personal intuition, right now, is leaning towards something that defies the usual grimness of post-apocalyptic. Maybe a game where the *survival* aspect isn't just about fighting mutants or raiding ruined cities, but about *rebuilding* beauty, finding joy, or even discovering strange, wonderful new forms of life and community in the aftermath. What if the "apocalypse" wasn't the end, but a radical new beginning? Perhaps a cooperative life-simulation or puzzle-adventure game where players are tasked with cultivating a new ecosystem, or fostering a unique post-human culture, rather than just fighting to stay alive. We could even have a competitive element where different factions are trying to build the most resilient or aesthetically pleasing new world. That's just a quick thought, of course, and I'll keep it to myself for now, as I want to hear what everyone else comes up with first. But it's a direction I find quite compelling – turning the genre on its head a bit.

So, strategy:
1.  Call a department-wide meeting.
2.  Clearly communicate the "free ideas" brief, emphasizing no genre or gameplay limitations beyond our initial departmental focus.
3.  Lead with open-ended questions to encourage broad thinking about player experience and world vision.
4.  Capture all ideas, no matter how outlandish, initially.
5.  After the initial brainstorm, we can then start to group, refine, and build upon the strongest concepts. That's where Tanaka and Watanabe's balanced and reflective approaches will really shine in structuring things. But first, pure, unadulterated creativity! This is going to be fun.
