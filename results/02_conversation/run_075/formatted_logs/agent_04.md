My approach will be simple: find Ito-san, grab a coffee, and just start talking. "Hey Ito-san, you got a minute? Just brainstorming for this new project, and my head's already spinning with crazy stuff. What's the wildest game idea you've ever had that never got made?" That kind of thing. I want to provoke some out-of-the-box thinking, and I'll do the same.

I'm thinking... we've got full freedom. "Modern Setting" means nothing. Let's go completely bonkers. What if we mash up genres? Like, a life simulation game, but it's set in a completely fantastical, absurd world where you're not just managing your life, but you're also a secret agent for an interdimensional agency, and sometimes you have to race snails or solve a cosmic puzzle to save the fabric of reality? No, wait, that's still a bit complex.

Okay, let's simplify. What if it's about *choice* and *consequence* in a really unique way? Not just a typical RPG with branching paths, but something where your small, seemingly insignificant actions throughout the day can have massive, unexpected ripple effects across a much larger, global or even cosmic narrative.

Let's call it... "Echoes of the Ordinary."
**Concept:** A seemingly mundane life simulation game where every tiny decision you make, from what you eat for breakfast to how you respond to a random stranger, creates subtle "echoes" that affect a hidden, grand narrative unfolding in the background. Players might not even realize they're part of something bigger until much later.
**Story:** You play as an average person in an average town. You go to work, interact with neighbors, pursue hobbies. But unknown to you, your town is a nexus point for parallel realities, or maybe a forgotten ancient power. Your choices, by shifting the collective 'vibration' of the town, slowly unravel or activate this larger mystery. You might eventually discover you're a reluctant hero, a crucial lynchpin, or even an accidental villain in a multi-layered story without ever picking up a sword or gun. The "villain" could just be a misunderstanding or a series of unfortunate events caused by seemingly neutral choices.
**Contents:**
*   **Gameplay Loop:** Daily routine (work, social, hobbies) mixed with emergent events.
*   **Choice System:** Not explicit "good/bad" choices, but subtle behavioral adjustments. Do you help the old lady with her groceries, or rush to an appointment? Do you complain about the weather, or find something positive? Each small interaction has a weight.
*   **Ripple Effect Visuals/Feedback:** Maybe tiny visual cues, like a butterfly effect animation, or shifts in the town's atmosphere (colors, music) that hint at the larger narrative building.
*   **Mini-games:** Tied to hobbies or work, but with unique twists that might affect the main "echo" system. For example, a cooking mini-game where the quality of your dish influences the mood of a pivotal NPC later.
*   **Unlocking the Grand Narrative:** As "echoes" accumulate, a meta-narrative starts to reveal itself through fragmented clues, mysterious characters, or even subtle changes in the game world's physics or rules.
*   **Multiple Endings:** Based on the cumulative effect of thousands of small decisions, leading to vastly different global outcomes.
**Slogan:** "Your Life. Their Destiny. Every Choice Echoes." or "The World Changes With Every Breath."
