Okay, let's try to flesh this out a bit.
**Concept:** "Echoes of the Forgotten." It's a narrative-driven, puzzle-adventure game where the player isn't a traditional detective, but a "Memory Weaver." Their role is to investigate historical "blurs" – periods where official records contradict oral traditions, or where significant events seem to have been deliberately erased or rewritten. The "crime" is the distortion of history, and the "culprit" might not be a single person, but a system, an ideology, or even a collective delusion.

**Story:** The protagonist, a skilled Memory Weaver, is called to a desolate, isolated town shrouded in perpetual mist. The town suffers from a peculiar malaise – its inhabitants are listless, uninspired, and seem to lack any vibrant cultural identity. The official history states the town was founded recently, but folk tales hint at a much older, vibrant past, now entirely suppressed. The player must uncover what happened to the town's original history and culture, and why it was buried. Was it a natural disaster misremembered? A tyrannical regime? Or something more ethereal, like a collective amnesia induced by a forgotten entity?

**Contents/Gameplay:**
The core gameplay would involve:
1.  **Memory Weaving:** Players navigate the town in two layers: the present, where they interact with the listless residents and analyze physical clues (dilapidated buildings, old objects), and the "Echo Realm," a semi-spiritual plane where fragments of the past manifest. In the Echo Realm, players can "weave" together conflicting memory fragments, visualize past events as spectral projections, and even subtly influence how certain echoes manifest to reveal new information.
2.  **Narrative Reconstruction:** Instead of interrogation, players "interview" environmental echoes, piece together fragmented dialogues from the past, and decipher symbolic imagery from ancient murals or forgotten rituals. Success in a "case" means restoring a piece of the town's lost memory, which gradually brings color and life back to the present-day town and its residents.
3.  **Moral Choices:** The game would present dilemmas. What if restoring a painful truth causes more suffering in the present? What if the "crime" was committed with good intentions? Players would have to decide which truths to prioritize, impacting the town's future.

**Slogan:** "Unravel the past, reclaim the future." Or perhaps, "History is a puzzle. What's been erased?"
**Key Selling Points:**
*   A unique twist on the "investigation" genre, focusing on historical truth and collective memory.
*   Deeply immersive narrative with emotionally resonant choices.
*   Innovative "Memory Weaving" gameplay mechanics that blend puzzle-solving with narrative exploration.
*   Atmospheric world-building with a mysterious, melancholic aesthetic that transforms as the story progresses.

Okay, I think this gives me something substantial to work with. It certainly steps outside the typical police procedural. It retains the core investigative element – seeking truth from fragments – but applies it to a broader, more philosophical scope.
