Okay, so here's a rough sketch for a concept that's brewing in my head. Let's call it "Echoes in the Net" for now, just a working title.

**Concept:** It's a modern-day mystery and deduction game, but with a unique twist. The player isn't a detective in the traditional sense, but more of an "archivist" or "digital cartographer" for historical data. The core idea is about preserving and understanding the forgotten stories of the internet age. Players delve into digital "relics" – old social media posts, archived websites, defunct messaging apps, encrypted files – to piece together narratives from the past. It's not about hacking or combat, but about forensic storytelling, empathy, and pattern recognition. The "modern setting" is essentially the digital landscape we're all swimming in, but seen through a historical lens, perhaps a few decades into the future from *our* present, where much of today's digital chatter has become archaeological data.

**Story:** The year is 2077. The internet as we know it has largely fractured into smaller, regulated "eco-nets." A powerful AI, designed to prevent information overload and historical revisionism, has been tasked with creating an definitive archive of humanity's digital past. However, even the AI struggles with the sheer volume and context of information from the mid-21st century. The player is a human "Echo Carver," someone with a unique intuition for digital forensics, brought in to "humanize" the archives. Their mission is to reconstruct specific, often poignant, individual stories that the AI can't fully grasp. These stories might be about a lost love, a forgotten scientific breakthrough, a hidden conspiracy, or even just the daily life of an ordinary person in a rapidly changing world. The overarching narrative could involve uncovering a pattern of intentional data erasure, hinting at a larger historical cover-up the AI is unknowingly trying to reconstruct.

**Contents/Gameplay:**
*   **Information Hub:** The player operates from a personalized digital workbench, equipped with various analytical tools.
*   **Digital Dig Sites:** Each mission involves exploring a "dig site," which is a cluster of interconnected old data. This could be a specific year, a location's archived public network, or a collection of a single individual's digital footprint.
*   **Data Fragments:** Players collect various "data fragments" – text snippets, corrupted images, audio files, social media conversations, even fragmented video. These are like puzzle pieces.
*   **Contextual Analysis:** Tools allow players to cross-reference data, analyze sentiment in text, identify hidden connections between users, and even reconstruct corrupted media using predictive algorithms.
*   **Narrative Reconstruction:** The goal is to build a coherent narrative from these fragments. This isn't just about finding the "truth," but understanding the *human perspective* within the data. Did two people argue or just have a playful banter? Was a public statement genuine or sarcastic?
*   **Empathy Engine:** A unique system that tracks how well the player understands the emotional and social context of the people whose "echoes" they are carving. Misinterpretations can lead to incomplete or even misleading archive entries, impacting the overarching story.
*   **Ethical Dilemmas:** As the player uncovers more personal data, they might face choices about what to include in the official archive and what to keep private, weighing historical accuracy against individual privacy.

**Slogan:** "Unearth Humanity's Digital Heartbeat." or "Their Stories. Your Legacy." I like the second one better actually. It feels more active.

**Key Selling Points:**
1.  **Unique Detective Experience:** Move beyond traditional crime scenes to the complex, often messy, landscape of digital history.
2.  **Emotional Storytelling:** Focus on deeply human, character-driven narratives, reconstructed from seemingly mundane data.
3.  **Engaging Deduction Mechanics:** A blend of puzzle-solving, forensic analysis, and contextual understanding. Not just finding clues, but interpreting them.
4.  **Reflective Gameplay:** Encourages thoughtful analysis and empathy rather than fast reflexes or combat.
5.  **Modern Relevance, Future Perspective:** Explores themes of data privacy, information overload, historical memory, and the human condition in the digital age, viewed from a slightly futuristic lens.

So, that's my initial brain dump. This really felt like the kind of idea I could get behind. Now, who to consult first and how to approach this task...
