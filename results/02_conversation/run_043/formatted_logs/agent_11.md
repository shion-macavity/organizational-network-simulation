- **Gameplay:** It would be a first-person life simulation/survival game with strong exploration and crafting elements. The "combat" wouldn't be about fighting enemies head-on, but more about understanding the ecosystem, outsmarting magical creatures, or using environmental puzzles to overcome threats.

- **Base Building:** Build a humble abode that slowly becomes more infused with the wild magic as you learn. Maybe your house itself starts to grow vines, glow faintly, or attract friendly forest spirits.
- **Social Element:** Maybe other outcast characters, also seeking the magic of the wilds, occasionally cross your path. You can choose to cooperate, trade, or even teach each other what you've learned. No big guilds, just small, intimate connections.
- **Art Style:** Definitely a vibrant, almost Ghibli-esque fantasy, but with a touch of melancholy and mystery. Hand-painted textures, dynamic lighting to highlight the magic in the environment.

**Key selling points:**
1. **A different kind of fantasy:** Focus on introspection, coexistence, and natural magic rather than epic quests and combat.
2. **Immersive life simulation:** Deep crafting, environmental interaction, and a living world that reacts to your presence and actions.
3. **Harmonious gameplay:** Solve problems through understanding and connection, not just brute force.
4. **Stunning, evolving world:** A beautiful, dynamic environment rich with secrets and subtle magic to uncover.
5. **Personal growth and discovery:** Your character's journey is about learning to belong, not just to survive.

Yeah, that's got some meat to it. It's got that fantasy root, but it branches out significantly into life-sim and survival, which feels fresh.
