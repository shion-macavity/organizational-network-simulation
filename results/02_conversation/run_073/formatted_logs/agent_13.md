It's about respecting the integrity of history while paradoxically needing to salvage what's left for the future. Players would have limited time jumps, each consuming a precious resource, making every mission incredibly vital and thoughtful.

For the **contents**, the core gameplay loop would involve temporal reconnaissance, using a specialized time-ship to identify key historical periods where specific species thrived, based on future ecological records. This would involve intricate puzzle mechanics, like understanding plant growth cycles or animal behaviors to get the sample without causing any paradoxes, almost like a microscopic life simulation. Finally, back in the future, these samples are brought to a grand biodome where the player cultivates them, nurturing them back to life to form a self-sustaining ecosystem. This phase would involve resource management and a bit of base-building, a serene gardening experience where success in the past directly impacts the future's vitality. Throughout this, there would be narrative elements exploring the weight of the mission and the ethics of manipulating life across time.
