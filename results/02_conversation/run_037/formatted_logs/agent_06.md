    *   I think Tanaka, with her 'Intuitive' work style, will be fantastic here. She's likely to throw out some really unconventional ideas that could spark something incredible. I'll make sure to give her plenty of space to speak first, to set an open tone.
    *   Suzuki, being 'Balanced' and younger, might bring a fresh, modern perspective that could really resonate with current trends, or combine existing elements in a unique way.
    *   The 'Reflective' members – Sato, Takahashi, Watanabe, Ito, and myself – will then be able to thoughtfully expand on these initial sparks, dig into the "why" and "how," and start to see potential connections. We'll be good at asking clarifying questions to flesh out even half-formed thoughts.

2.  **Guiding the Brainstorming (My Role):**
    *   I'll start by briefly re-explaining the project's openness, emphasizing that while "Post-Apocalyptic" is our department's general area, we're not bound by typical tropes. We can mix genres, explore unusual settings within that theme, or even push the boundaries of what "post-apocalyptic" means. Maybe it's a post-apocalypse of *ideas*, not just civilization? Or a psychological one?
    *   I'll specifically ask them to think about:
        *   **Core Concept/Hook:** What's the one thing that makes our game unique?
        *   **Story/World:** What kind of post-apocalyptic world are we in? What happened? What's the prevailing mood?
        *   **Player Experience:** What do we want players to *feel*? (Hope, despair, dread, curiosity, mastery, connection?)
        *   **Gameplay Loop (Broad Strokes):** Without getting bogged down in mechanics, what do players *do* in this world? Is it about rebuilding, surviving, exploring, solving a mystery, competing?
    *   I'll ensure everyone gets a chance to contribute and that we capture all ideas, no matter how brief, on a whiteboard or digital document. We can categorize them loosely as we go, but the main goal is quantity and diversity first.

3.  **Post-Brainstorming Reflection & Refinement (My Internal Process & Follow-up):**
    *   After the initial session, I'll take some time alone to review all the generated ideas. As a reflective leader, I need to process everything, look for patterns, and identify promising threads.
    *   I'll try to group similar ideas, identify potential conflicts, and see where we might have gaps.
    *   Then, I'll probably schedule a follow-up meeting, or perhaps even break the team into smaller groups, to start refining the most promising concepts. At that point, we can dive deeper into things like specific content ideas, potential slogans, and clear key selling points for a few select concepts.

My main concern is making sure we don't just churn out another generic "scavenge and survive" game. The open brief is an opportunity to be truly innovative, and I want to leverage my team's collective brainpower to do that. This initial all-hands brainstorming is crucial for setting that tone and getting those creative juices flowing.
