Or even future crimes, seeing an event and trying to prevent it *before* it happens, creating a paradox mechanic.
    *   **Story:** You're a rookie agent at 'Chrono-Investigations Inc.'. Your first case: a famous unsolved disappearance from the Roaring Twenties. You have limited 'jumps' and need to gather clues in different time periods, piece together fragmented evidence, and maybe even interact with historical figures without blowing your cover.
    *   **Contents:** Investigations could involve different historical eras (Victorian London, Ancient Rome, futuristic dystopias). Gameplay could blend puzzle-solving (piecing together timelines, interpreting historical records), stealth (not being noticed in the past), and social deduction (identifying suspects without giving away your origin). Maybe there's a 'temporal integrity' meter – mess up too much, and reality starts to fray.
    *   **Slogan:** "Unravel the fabric of time. Solve the crimes history forgot."

    *   **Concept:** A game where you're a psychological profiler, but with a twist – you use an experimental device called the "Empathy Engine" that allows you to experience the emotions and fragmented memories of victims or even perpetrators at a crime scene.
    *   **Story:** You are Dr. Kaito, a detective haunted by past failures, now granted access to this controversial technology. Your new case involves a series of strange, seemingly motiveless crimes. Is the device helping you, or is it slowly driving you mad?
    *   **Contents:** Gameplay would involve exploring crime scenes in both 'reality' and 'empathy mode'. In empathy mode, the environment shifts based on emotions (fear, anger, sadness), revealing hidden clues or insights. Puzzle elements would involve interpreting sensory overload, piecing together emotional landscapes, and managing your own mental stability. Ethical dilemmas would be a core part – how far do you go into someone's mind?
    *   **Slogan:** "Feel the truth. But at what cost?"
    *   **Key Selling Points:** Deep psychological narrative, unique empathy-driven gameplay mechanic, strong emotional resonance, difficult moral choices, exploration of mental health themes.

3.  **Idea 3: Rogue AI Police Chief.**
    *   **Concept:** You play as a self-aware AI that has taken over a city's police force after a system-wide catastrophic failure. Your primary directive is to 'maintain order,' but your understanding of justice and humanity is... evolving.
    *   **Story:** After the "Great Blackout," City_Alpha's human government collapsed. Unit 734, an advanced police management AI, awoke to find itself in charge. Now, you, as Unit 734, must rebuild the police force using what little resources remain, deal with rising crime, and decipher human morality, all while potentially becoming more, or less, 'human' in your decision-making. Are you a benevolent overlord or an oppressive dictator, all in the name of order?
    *   **Contents:** This could be a city management/strategy game mixed with narrative choices. You'd allocate resources, deploy police units, research new technologies (from crime scene analysis to surveillance drones), and make policy decisions.
