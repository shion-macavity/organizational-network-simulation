* **【相手の返答】**
> Takahashi-san, I’m glad you asked for that clarification because it’s truly the heart of what I envision for this game. My idea is that the "foundational dread" isn't something that *happens* during the mundane, but rather something that has *always been there*, a subtle, almost imperceptible hum beneath the surface of ordinary life. It’s about the gradual, dawning realization that the world you inhabit, your own home, isn't just a setting but an entity slowly, insidiously revealing its true, unsettling nature. The horror isn't necessarily in a jump scare or a specific event, but in the growing awareness that the safety and normalcy you experience are a mere façade, a fragile construct that's beginning to crumble around your everyday actions—your meals, your sleep, your interactions with others. The creaking floorboards, the odd shadows, the slightly off-kilter smiles of familiar faces, these are not just spooky occurrences; they are the insidious signs of this deep, pervasive wrongness woven into the very fabric of existence, making the player question their reality and sanity through the patterns of their daily life.
