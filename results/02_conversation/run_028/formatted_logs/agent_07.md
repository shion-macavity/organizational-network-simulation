How about this: Instead of the typical grand space opera or alien invasion, what if we went with something more intimate, but still very sci-fi? A "quiet" sci-fi, almost.

Let's imagine a game called "Echoes of the Ark."
**Concept:** It's set hundreds of years in the future, humanity has fled a dying Earth on a massive generation ship, an "Ark." The ship has been traveling so long that the original mission and even the memory of Earth have faded for most of the current inhabitants. The game isn't about finding a new planet, or fighting aliens. It's about maintaining this ancient, decaying vessel while rediscovering the lost history of humanity and the true purpose of the Ark.
**Story:** You play as a Custodian, a member of a guild responsible for the ship's continued function. You discover a series of cryptic data logs and forgotten sections of the ship that reveal the Ark isn't just a transport – it's a living archive, and its purpose is far more complex and emotionally resonant than simple colonization. The ship itself is starting to "forget," experiencing system failures that mirror humanity's own amnesia. Your goal is to literally repair the ship's memory, piece by piece, both mechanical and historical, to ensure humanity's legacy endures, even if there's no destination left.
**Contents:**
*   **Exploration:** Navigate the vast, labyrinthine corridors and forgotten sectors of the Ark. Each sector could have its own unique ecosystem, cultures, or forgotten technologies.
*   **Puzzle/Investigation:** Decipher ancient data logs, repair broken systems through environmental puzzles, and reconstruct historical events. Think "Myst" meets a touch of "Alien: Isolation" (but without the monster, just the oppressive atmosphere and a sense of decay).
*   **Life Simulation/Resource Management:** Manage the ship's dwindling resources, keep critical systems online (power, life support, food synthesis), and deal with the social dynamics of the different communities living on board. Your actions might lead to different outcomes for these communities.
*   **Narrative Focus:** Heavy emphasis on storytelling through environmental clues, recovered data, and interactions with NPCs who might have their own theories or agendas about the Ark's past and future.
**Slogan:** "Before we can find our future, we must remember our past." Or perhaps, "The voyage isn't over until the last memory fades."
**Key Selling Points:**
1.  **Unique Sci-Fi Narrative:** A departure from typical space combat or colonization, focusing on existential themes of memory, legacy, and the true meaning of a journey.
2.  **Atmospheric Exploration & Mystery:** A vast, decaying generation ship as a "character" in itself, full of secrets to uncover.
3.  **Engaging Puzzle-Driven Gameplay:** Challenges that are integrated with the story, requiring critical thinking rather than just reflexes.
4.  **Emotional Depth:** A story that makes players ponder humanity's place in the cosmos and the importance of preserving knowledge.

Yeah, I think that's a good starting point. It touches on sci-fi but leans heavily into mystery, puzzle, and narrative, which I personally find more compelling sometimes.
