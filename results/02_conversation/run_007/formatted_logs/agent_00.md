Okay, I'm starting to get a flicker of an idea. What if it's a game set in a near-future, slightly dystopian but still recognizable modern city, where personal data and privacy are paramount, but also constantly under threat? And instead of being a hero fighting against the system, you're an ordinary citizen trying to live your life, but you discover a unique ability to 'read' or 'sense' the emotional resonance left behind by people and events in places.

This could lead to a mystery game, but not a typical detective one. More of an empathic exploration. You're not looking for clues, but for *feelings*, *memories*, *echoes*. The story could involve unraveling a quiet conspiracy or helping people find closure.

So, for **story**: The protagonist is an average person in Neo-Veridia, a sleek, hyper-connected city. They start experiencing strange 'echoes' – residual emotions or fragments of past moments that linger in specific locations. They soon realize this isn't a glitch in their brain, but a unique sensitivity. They get drawn into a series of interconnected personal stories, from a lost love letter's final moments, to the despair of a corporate whistleblower, to the joy of a forgotten street artist. Gradually, they uncover a larger narrative about how the city's seemingly perfect data-driven infrastructure is inadvertently suppressing or distorting human connection and memory, and a small, underground group is trying to restore it.

The **concept** would be "Empathy as a Superpower in a Desensitized World." It's about reconnecting with the human element in an increasingly digital and often isolating modern existence.

For **contents**:
*   **Gameplay:** It would be an exploration-focused RPG/Adventure game. Players would navigate the city environments – bustling plazas, quiet alleyways, sterile corporate buildings, vibrant markets. The core mechanic would be activating their 'echo-sight' to perceive these emotional remnants.
*   **Puzzles:** Instead of traditional inventory puzzles, players would "piece together" emotional narratives by collecting these echoes, understanding different perspectives, and making choices about how to interpret or act upon them. For instance, an echo might show a moment of conflict, and the player needs to find other echoes from different participants or times to understand the full context.
*   **Progression:** As the protagonist uses their ability, it could evolve. They might be able to 'project' a feeling to calm a situation, or 're-experience' a memory more vividly to find a hidden detail. There would be dialogue choices that impact relationships with NPCs and the unfolding story.
*   **Art Style:** A clean, slightly futuristic aesthetic for the base city, but when 'echo-sight' is active, the world could take on a dreamlike, impressionistic quality with vibrant color overlays representing different emotions (e.g., warm for joy, cool for sadness, red for anger).

**Slogan:** "Feel What's Unseen. Heal What's Broken." or maybe something more active like "Beyond the Glitch, Discover the Echo." I like the second one better for a game. "Beyond the Glitch, Discover the Echo."

**Key Selling Points:**
1.  **Unique Empathy-Driven Gameplay:** Experience a narrative adventure where emotion and intuition are your primary tools, rather than combat or stealth.
2.  **Rich, Interconnected Stories:** Unravel a tapestry of personal tales and a larger conspiracy, offering deep emotional engagement and moral choices.
3.  **Stunning Near-Future City:** Explore a beautifully rendered urban landscape that dynamically changes visually to reflect the unseen emotional undercurrents.
4.  **A Fresh Take on Modern Dystopia:** No guns, no grand heroes, just a human journey to reconnect in a technologically advanced, yet emotionally fragmented, world.

Okay, that's a solid initial idea. Now, 'who to consult first'?

Given my reflective nature, I've spent a good amount of time thinking this through alone. Now I need to see if these ideas resonate.
