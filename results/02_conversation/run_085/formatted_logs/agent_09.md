## ■ ターン 0: 初期状態
* **【初期思考】**
> Alright, time to get the creative juices flowing for this new project! My department is "Superhero Game Planning," but the brief said we can really go wild and incorporate anything, even completely different genres. That's actually pretty exciting. As an intuitive person, I love these open-ended challenges; it gives me a chance to really think outside the box and connect some unexpected dots.

So, superhero game... but what *kind* of superhero game? Everyone always thinks of flying around, punching villains, saving the world. We've seen that a million times. I want to do something that makes people go, "Huh, I didn't expect *that*!"

What if we explore the idea of powers, but not in a battle-centric way? What if the "super" part is more about... potential, or influence, rather than brute force or laser beams?

Here's an idea that just popped into my head: what if the game is about a group of people who *used to be* superheroes, but now they're trying to live normal lives? Or maybe they're just starting to discover their powers, but they're not the "heroic" type, they're more... community-focused?

Okay, let's refine that. How about a life simulation game, but set in a neighborhood where everyone secretly has a minor superpower? Not like, world-ending powers, but things like: being able to perfectly grow any plant, or subtly influence people's moods, or always know when someone needs help, or see glimpses of the very near future. The game isn't about fighting a big bad supervillain; it's about using these unique "superpowers" to build up their community, solve everyday problems, and help each other.

Imagine the story: you move into this quirky, slightly rundown neighborhood called "Havenwood." You don't know it yet, but everyone here has a unique, often subtle, superpower. Your own power might be something like "empathy amplification" or "minor matter manipulation" (like, you can fix small broken things with a touch, or make ingredients taste *perfect*). The goal isn't to fight crime, but to improve Havenwood. Maybe the local park is neglected, or a new business is struggling, or an elderly resident needs help with their garden. You'd use your power, and cooperate with other residents who have complementary powers, to solve these challenges.

The core concept would be "Community Building with a Super Twist." It's about how small, everyday acts, amplified by unique abilities, can create something truly extraordinary.

Contents-wise, players could:
*   **Discover and hone their unique superpower:** Each playthrough could offer different starting powers, or a choice from a selection, leading to replayability. As you use your power, it could "level up" in subtle ways, unlocking new applications.
*   **Interact with a diverse cast of characters:** Each with their own personality, story, and *secret* superpower. For example, the baker might have "perfect ingredient recall," always knowing the exact recipe, or the librarian might have "knowledge absorption," remembering everything they read instantly.
*   **Engage in various "missions":** These wouldn't be combat missions, but more like community quests. "Help Mrs. Henderson grow her prize-winning roses" (using your plant-growing power), "Organize a block party to lift spirits" (using your empathy amplification), "Renovate the old community center" (cooperating with someone who has "minor structural reinforcement" power).
*   **Personalize their home and avatar:** Standard life-sim elements, but maybe with some super-themed decorations or a subtle nod to your powers.
*   **Uncover the mystery of Havenwood:** Why do all these people have powers? Is there a reason they're drawn to this particular neighborhood? Maybe there's a gentle overarching narrative about a past event or a hidden truth about their powers.

The slogan? How about: "Havenwood: Where Every Act of Kindness is a Superpower." Or, "Build Your Community. Unleash Your Everyday Hero." I kind of like the second one because it ties back to the "superhero" department but spins it differently.

Key selling points:
1.  **A Fresh Take on Superpowers:** Moves beyond combat to focus on cooperation, community, and personal growth.
2.  **Charming Life Simulation Gameplay:** Appeals to fans of relaxed, engaging simulation games, but with a unique twist.
3.  **Endless Discovery and Replayability:** Randomized powers, diverse character interactions, and evolving community challenges ensure each playthrough feels fresh.
4.  **Positive and Uplifting Experience:** Focuses on helping others and building a better world, providing a feel-good gaming experience.

I think this concept really hits the "think outside the box" directive while still having a clear connection to "superhero" in an unexpected way. It definitely leans into the cooperative and life simulation genres.

Now, who should I talk to first about this? Yamaguchi-san, our leader, will eventually summarize everything, so she'll need the polished idea. But before that, I think bouncing this off someone else in the department would be really useful. Maybe Kobayashi-san, since he's also intuitive. I bet he'd have some great spontaneous feedback or even ideas to expand on this. Or Sasaki-san, she's closer to my age, maybe she'd get the vibe. Nakamura-san might appreciate the community aspect given her balanced work style, but I think the initial brainstorm would benefit from another intuitive person. Yeah, I think I'll want to run this by Kobayashi-san first to see if he thinks it has legs before I formalize it too much. His intuitive brain might just spark something even better!
