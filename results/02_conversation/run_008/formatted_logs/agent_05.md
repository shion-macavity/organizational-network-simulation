* **【初期思考】**
> Okay, let's get this brain working. New project, completely free ideas, that's exciting! As an intuitive person, I really thrive on this kind of open-ended brief. "Modern Setting" is just a guideline, which is great, because sometimes those broad genres can feel a bit restrictive. I can definitely see myself going a bit outside the box here.

Here’s an initial spark of an idea: How about a game called "Echoes of Tomorrow."

**The Story & Concept:**
The game is set in a near-future, beautifully stylized modern city – think vibrant neon, bustling pedestrian areas, but also quiet, hidden corners. The core concept revolves around the player character running a very unique kind of "archive" or "restoration service." It’s not just for physical items; it's for *lost memories* or *fragmented stories* belonging to the city's inhabitants.
The city has a peculiar phenomenon: sometimes, people experience transient "memory echoes" – brief, vivid flashes of moments, feelings, or even whispers of conversations that aren't their own, but belong to the city's collective past or even parallel present moments. These echoes can be confusing, sometimes poignant, sometimes a little spooky. Our player character has a unique ability to not just *perceive* these echoes more clearly, but to also help "synthesize" or "reconstruct" them into coherent narratives for the people who experience them.
Each "client" who comes to our establishment has experienced an echo, and the player's job is to gently piece together the story behind it. This involves exploring parts of the city connected to the echo, gathering subtle clues (environmental details, overheard conversations, forgotten digital fragments), and then, through a puzzle-like mechanic, assembling the fragmented memories into a complete, emotional narrative. As the player restores more memories for different individuals, a larger, overarching mystery about the city itself begins to unfold – perhaps about its founding, a forgotten event, or even a hidden truth about the nature of these echoes.

**Contents & Gameplay:**
*   **The Archive Hub:** The player's main base of operations, a cozy yet futuristic "memory restoration" café/archive. Here, they'll interact with clients, review gathered clues, and piece together the memory puzzles. Customization of this hub could be a nice touch.
*   **Urban Exploration:** The city itself is a character. Players explore different districts, each with its own vibe, architecture, and hidden stories. Movement would be smooth and engaging, maybe parkour-lite or using futuristic personal transport.
*   **Memory Reconstruction Puzzles:** These would be the core gameplay loop. Imagine a holographic interface where fragments of sensory input (visuals, sounds, emotions) need to be arranged chronologically or thematically to reveal the full memory.
*   **Interpersonal Storytelling:** Every client has a unique story, ranging from heartwarming to melancholic. Successfully restoring memories deepens the connection with NPCs, unlocking side quests or more profound revelations.
*   **The Grand City Mystery:** The big "story arc" would be uncovering why these echoes are happening and what truth about the city they point to. This could involve ancient tech, a forgotten community, or even an interdimensional phenomenon hidden beneath the modern facade.
*   **Cooperative (Optional):** Imagine if a second player could join as an "assistant archivist," helping gather clues, or offering a different perspective on the memory puzzles. Or, even simpler, asynchronous cooperation where players can leave "resonance points" in the city for others to find, hinting at echoes.

**Slogan:**
"Echoes of Tomorrow: Reclaim What Was Lost. Remember Who You Are."

**Key Selling Points:**
1.  **Emotionally Rich Narrative:** A deep, character-driven story focusing on human connection, memory, and the unseen threads that tie a city together.
2.  **Unique "Memory Puzzle" Gameplay:** Innovative mechanics centered around piecing together fragmented experiences, offering a fresh take on puzzle-solving.
3.  **Vibrant, Immersive City:** A stunningly realized near-future urban environment, begging to be explored for its beauty and its hidden secrets.
4.  **Cozy Yet Thought-Provoking Atmosphere:** A game that encourages contemplation and discovery over aggression, perfect for players seeking a different kind of challenge.

So, that's my initial gut feeling for a direction. It feels fresh and allows for a lot of creative freedom while still touching on the "modern setting" in a meaningful way.

Now, who would I consult first? My first instinct is to bounce this off someone who can help me refine the narrative and emotional impact, but also someone who can think about the practicalities of making the *puzzle* elements
