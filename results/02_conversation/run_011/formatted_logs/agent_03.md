**Concept:** It's a "Post-Prophecy Life Simulation RPG." The world has been saved, but life goes on. What do you do when the epic quest is over, but the world is still brimming with residual magic, displaced monsters, and broken economies? You play as a regular citizen trying to make a living, build a community, and find your own small purpose in a world that's simultaneously recovering and trying to redefine itself *after* the grand narrative.

**Story:** You start as a young person (or maybe a grizzled veteran returning from the final war) in a small, struggling town. The hero of legend vanished after their victory, leaving behind a power vacuum and a changed landscape. Your personal story isn't about saving the world, but about growing your own farm, opening a shop selling repurposed monster parts, becoming an archivist of forgotten spells, or even a local peacekeeper mediating disputes between goblins who've decided to integrate and grumpy old dwarves. As you interact with the world, you uncover smaller, more personal mysteries – a forgotten relic from the hero, a minor curse affecting a neighbor's crops, a budding cult trying to *recreate* the dark lord just for kicks. It's about finding the extraordinary in the ordinary, post-apocalyptic fantasy life.

**Contents:**
*   **Gameplay Loop:** Day-night cycle, seasonal changes affecting resources and events. Core loop involves resource gathering, crafting, trading, social interaction (building relationships, resolving local quests), and exploration of nearby "dungeons" or "ruins" that are now just dangerous but resource-rich areas.
*   **Magic System:** Magic isn't for epic battles, but for utility. Enchanting tools for better harvests, creating light, quick travel, maybe even mundane things like magically cooling drinks or speeding up construction.
*   **Characters:** A diverse cast of NPCs, including retired adventurers dealing with PTSD, reformed goblins trying to open bakeries, disillusioned mages working as plumbers, and children who only know peace. Each has their own stories and needs.
*   **Choices & Consequences:** Your actions impact your local community and reputation. Helping one faction might alienate another. You could build up your town, contribute to larger regional projects, or focus solely on your own prosperity.
*   **No "Big Bad":** The primary conflict isn't a single antagonist, but the challenges of rebuilding, adaptation, and finding meaning in a world that no longer needs a hero.

**Slogan:** "The hero went home. Now it's your turn to live." Or, maybe something a bit punchier: "After the epic. Before the next one." I like that second one, it hints at both recovery and potential.

**Key Selling Points:**
1.  **Unique "Post-Epic" Setting:** Experience a fantasy world *after* the typical hero's journey, focusing on reconstruction and everyday life.
2.  **Player-Driven Micro-Narratives:** Forge your own story of personal growth and community building, not world-saving.
3.  **Mundane Magic & Reimagined Fantasy:** See magic integrated into daily life and iconic fantasy races dealing with very human problems.
4.  **Cozy Exploration & Crafting:** A relaxing yet engaging loop of gathering, creating, and discovering the smaller wonders of a vast, changed world.
