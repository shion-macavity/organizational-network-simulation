My approach needs to be very open initially. I'm not going to come in with *my* fully formed idea and dictate it. That kills creativity. Instead, I'll frame it as a blank canvas. I'll probably kick off with a general brainstorming session, maybe even a few. We can start by just throwing out keywords, feelings, core gameplay loops we enjoy, or even just interesting "what if" scenarios. We need to explore different gameplay styles too – action, RPG, puzzle, even something like a life sim in a sci-fi setting could be really unique.

I'm thinking, for the core concept, something that leans into the "humanity's future" aspect of sci-fi, but perhaps with a twist. Not just space battles or saving the galaxy from aliens. Maybe something more personal, or philosophical, wrapped in an engaging gameplay loop. For instance, what if we combine a deeply narrative, choice-driven RPG with some elements of exploration and resource management, but set it within a post-apocalyptic, bio-engineered world that's slowly re-terraforming itself? The "SF" part could be the advanced, decaying technology and the strange, mutated flora and fauna. The "other elements" could be the survival and crafting aspects, or even a mystery element about what caused the apocalypse.

Let's say, the story revolves around a small, scattered community of survivors centuries after a technological catastrophe. They live in pockets of safe zones, barely clinging to advanced tech that's now mostly incomprehensible. The player character is an explorer, a "Scavenger," whose job is to venture into the overgrown ruins of the old world, not just for resources, but for *knowledge*. They discover a dormant AI network that hints at a way to restore the planet, but it requires them to make difficult moral choices, like sacrificing one restored zone to power another, or deciding which factions get access to limited, life-changing technology.

The *contents* would involve exploring vast, procedurally generated or hand-crafted biomes – think dense, glowing jungles growing out of skyscraper ruins, deserts where ancient solar farms are slowly being reclaimed by sand, and deep, dark underground facilities where the remnants of the old world's secrets lie. Gameplay could involve stealth, puzzle-solving to reactivate old tech, resource gathering to upgrade your gear and base, and maybe some light combat against mutated creatures or rival scavenger gangs. The core loop would be exploration, discovery, choice, and then bringing back findings to your community, seeing the impact of your decisions.

For a slogan... something that captures the blend of hope and decay, exploration and difficult choices. Maybe "Echoes of Tomorrow, Choices of Today." Or "Reclaim the Future. One Decision at a Time."

Key selling points? Definitely the deep, branching narrative and moral choices. The unique blend of post-apocalyptic survival with sophisticated, decaying sci-fi elements. The sense of mystery and discovery in a richly detailed, recovering world. And the impact of player choice on the world and its inhabitants, making each playthrough feel truly personal.

Now, who to consult first? Well, after that initial team brainstorm, I'm going to want to hear everyone's raw ideas. Then we can start refining. I'll probably set up a whiteboard session, or an online collaborative board, where we can just dump everything. I'm thinking of encouraging a "yes, and" approach initially, to keep the ideas flowing freely without judgment.

I also need to consider if reaching out to the other department would be beneficial. They might have a completely different perspective, especially if their genre is something wildly different. If we're truly thinking "outside the box," getting input from someone not steeped in SF could spark something truly unique. I'll keep that option open. After we've hammered out some initial concepts internally, if we feel stuck or want a completely fresh pair of eyes, I might consider an informal chat with someone from the other team, just to bounce ideas around. It's optional, but it could be a real game-changer for innovative thinking. No point in isolating ourselves if we don't have to.

So, first step: set the stage for maximum creativity and inclusivity within our own team. Then, we build on that. I'm looking forward to seeing what everyone comes up with. This could be really special.
