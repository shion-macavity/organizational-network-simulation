**Story/Concept:**
The player character moves to a picturesque, isolated village called Evergreen Valley, looking for a peaceful escape from city life. At first, everything is idyllic: friendly villagers, beautiful landscapes, satisfying routines of farming, fishing, and crafting. But as time passes, subtle anomalies start to appear. Villagers sometimes repeat strange phrases, their expressions flicker unnaturally, objects in your house rearrange themselves when you're not looking, or you catch glimpses of figures in the periphery of your vision that vanish instantly. The seasons become subtly warped, the crops yield unsettling produce, and the local wildlife seems to observe you with uncanny intelligence. The goal isn't just to survive, but to uncover the unsettling truth behind Evergreen Valley – is it a curse, a eldritch entity slowly consuming the reality of the village, or is the player character slowly losing their mind?

**Contents:**
*   **Daily life mechanics:** Farming, foraging, fishing, crafting, building relationships with villagers (who become progressively more unsettling).
*   **Gradual psychological horror:** Environmental storytelling with subtle changes – wilting flora, strange symbols appearing, distorted sounds, unsettling dreams.
*   **Uncovering lore:** Finding cryptic notes, ancient texts, or hidden areas that hint at the village's true nature.
*   **Moral choices:** Decisions that impact the player's sanity or the village's descent into madness. For instance, do you report strange occurrences, risk being dismissed, or worse? Do you participate in bizarre village rituals that promise bountiful harvests but feel deeply wrong?
*   **No combat:** The horror comes from the atmosphere and the creeping realization that your safe haven is a trap. Survival is about maintaining your sanity and understanding what's happening.
*   **Dynamic village state:** The village itself transforms based on player actions and the progression of the hidden horror. Buildings might decay, the sky might change color, the music might become dissonant.

**Slogan:**
"Your perfect escape... is a perfect nightmare."
Or maybe, "Tend your garden. Lose your mind."

**Key Selling Points:**
1.  **Unique blend of genres:** A cozy life simulation game infused with deep psychological and cosmic horror.
2.  **Unnerving atmosphere:** Focus on subtle dread, uncanny elements, and a creeping sense of unease rather than cheap jump scares.
3.  **Engaging mystery:** Unravel the disturbing secrets of Evergreen Valley and the nature of the pervasive evil.
4.  **High replayability:** Multiple endings based on player choices and investigative paths, leading to different forms of despair or fragile hope.
5.  **Relatable setting, twisted:** Leveraging the comfort and familiarity of life-sim games to amplify the unsettling horror elements.

Yeah, I think something like that could really stand out. It's horror, but it's also something completely different. I'm excited to hear what everyone else thinks. I’ll start jotting down some more specific ideas for those unsettling villagers and events, so I can bring them to the brainstorm.
