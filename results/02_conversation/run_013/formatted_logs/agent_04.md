**Story Idea:** Players are "Scribes of Time," guardians of knowledge within a fantastical, ever-expanding Library that exists *between* historical eras. One day, the Grand Archives, which hold the true history of the world, start corrupting. Books are shifting, timelines are unraveling, and historical figures are appearing out of their proper eras. Players need to work together to restore the true timelines before reality itself collapses. It's not just about reading; it's about actively *participating* in these fractured histories.

**Concept:** A cooperative mystery/puzzle adventure game with a life-simulation layer in a magical historical library hub. Players choose a "scribe" archetype, each specializing in a different kind of knowledge or skill (e.g., a "Cartographer" who understands space and navigation, a "Linguist" who deciphers ancient texts, a "Scholar of Customs" who understands social interactions across eras).

**Contents (Gameplay):**
*   **Library Hub (Life Simulation):** This is where players start. They can customize their scribe's quarters, research historical events, craft tools from artifacts found in missions, and interact with other Scribes (NPCs and other players). They could also participate in daily library tasks like organizing shelves or transcribing scrolls to earn "Knowledge Points."
*   **Time-Warp Missions (Mystery/Puzzle/Cooperative):** Teams of 2-4 players are dispatched to "Anomalies" – specific historical moments that are breaking down. For example, suddenly Vikings are trying to sail across the Sahara Desert, or ancient Egyptians are building pyramids using Roman engineering techniques. Players must investigate clues, solve puzzles based on historical knowledge (or the lack thereof), interact with displaced historical figures, and subtly "correct" the timeline without being noticed. Maybe they need to find a specific artifact that's out of place, or help a historical figure remember their true purpose. Each anomaly is a self-contained story.
*   **"Truth Weaving" Mechanic:** A core puzzle mechanic where players reconstruct fragmented historical documents or events by correctly placing pieces of information, visual cues, or cultural practices into a chronological or logical order. If they fail too often, the anomaly gets worse, making the mission harder.
*   **Boss Battles (Conceptual):** Not traditional combat, but intellectual "battles" against powerful "Paradox Entities" that are feeding off the timeline instability. These would be grand, multi-stage puzzles requiring coordinated efforts from the whole team, using all their learned historical knowledge and specialized skills.

**Slogan:** "Unravel History. Reclaim Reality." Or maybe, "Where every page holds a paradox." I like the first one better, it's more direct.

**Key Selling Points:**
1.  **Unique Blend of Genres:** A fresh take on cooperative gaming that combines historical mystery, complex puzzles, and engaging life-sim elements.
2.  **Immersive Historical Exploration:** Experience history not just as facts, but as vibrant, interconnected narratives, with opportunities to interact with famous and lesser-known historical events and figures in unexpected ways.
3.  **Deep Cooperative Gameplay:** Success hinges on teamwork, communication, and leveraging diverse character skills, fostering a truly collaborative experience.
4.  **Endless Replayability:** Procedurally generated anomalies and a vast, expanding library hub ensure that no two playthroughs are exactly alike, offering new mysteries to solve and knowledge to uncover.
5.  **A Story-Driven Experience:** A compelling overarching narrative about preserving reality itself, full of intrigue, discovery, and the magic of knowledge.
