* **【初期思考】**
> Alright, so, new game idea, totally free-form, doesn't even have to stick strictly to historical. But, you know, "Historical Game Planning" is our department, so it'd be a shame not to use that as a launching pad, right? My gut feeling is we should lean into what we know, but twist it completely. No point in making just *another* historical simulation. We need something fresh, something that sparks curiosity.

Okay, my first thought, just off the top of my head, is to combine history with something completely unexpected. How about a game where the past isn't just... the past? What if history itself is a living, breathing entity, constantly being written and rewritten, and you're part of that process, not just observing it?

Let's call it "Echoes of Aeon."

The core **concept** would be a blend of historical investigation, narrative puzzle-solving, and a touch of time manipulation. Not like a time machine, more like influencing the ripples of time. The player isn't a warrior or a ruler, but a "Temporal Weaver," someone who can perceive the multi-layered nature of history and mend its broken threads, or perhaps subtly re-weave them.

For the **story**, imagine this: Major historical events, like the fall of Rome, the rise of the samurai, the Enlightenment – they didn't happen exactly as we know them. There are subtle "anomalies," paradoxes, or even intentional distortions introduced by an unseen force throughout history. These distortions cause "temporal echoes" that manifest as slight shifts in reality, forgotten figures, or unexplained phenomena. You, the Temporal Weaver, wake up with this unique ability, perhaps cursed with memories of timelines that no longer exist. Your mission is to uncover the source of these distortions and decide whether to restore the original timeline, or embrace the changes for a potentially better future. It’s about understanding the intricate dance between cause and effect, and free will versus destiny.

Now, for **contents**. I'm thinking a narrative-driven adventure game, with strong elements of mystery and puzzle-solving.
Players would explore historically inspired environments – say, a bustling medieval market, a Roman villa, a 19th-century Parisian salon – but with an overlay of these "temporal echoes." You might find an artifact that shouldn't exist in that era, or encounter a person whose story seems to conflict with recorded history.
Gameplay would involve:
1.  **Investigation:** Collecting historical records, interacting with NPCs (both historical figures and common folk), and observing the environment for clues.
2.  **Echo-Sight:** A special ability to see "ghosts" of alternate timelines or past events, offering glimpses into what *should have been* or *could be*.
3.  **Thread Manipulation:** Instead of direct time travel, you'd use a more abstract system to subtly influence events. This could be through making a pivotal decision for an NPC, solving a dilemma that changes a local outcome, or even just leaving a specific item in a specific place to trigger a butterfly effect. This isn't about changing a grand event like "preventing the assassination of Caesar" directly, but rather influencing the *narrative* around it, perhaps ensuring a different outcome for his successors or for the city itself.
4.  **Moral Choices:** Your actions would have consequences, creating branching narratives. Do you restore a harsh but "correct" timeline, or do you allow a more benevolent but "distorted" one to persist? There's no clear good or evil, just different shades of historical truth.

The **slogan**... something impactful, right? How about: "History is not written. It is woven." Or maybe, "Mend the past. Weave the future." I like "History is not written. It is woven." It captures that active, creative essence.

And for the **key selling points**:
1.  **A Fresh Take on History:** It’s not just learning history; it's *engaging* with it in a profound and interactive way.
2.  **Deep, Branching Narratives:** Player choices truly matter, leading to unique historical outcomes and a highly replayable experience.
3.  **Intriguing Mystery & Puzzle Gameplay:** Perfect for players who love to uncover secrets and piece together complex narratives.
4.  **Visually Stunning & Immersive Historical Worlds:** Explore meticulously crafted environments that blend historical accuracy with a touch of the fantastical.

Okay, that feels like a solid foundation. My brain is buzzing with possibilities now.
