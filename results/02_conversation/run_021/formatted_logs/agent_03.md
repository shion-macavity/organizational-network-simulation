I'd brainstorm different themes that could be universally appealing but haven't been fully explored in games, or at least not in a novel way. Maybe something about community building, or exploring the unknown, or even a different take on personal growth and achievement that isn't just about combat or speed. I'd probably jot down a lot of keywords and concepts on a notepad, just letting my mind wander.

After I've got a decent pool of initial concepts, maybe three or four distinct directions, that's when I'd start thinking about "who to consult first." Given my reflective nature, I think the most productive first consultation for me would be with someone who also appreciates a thorough approach, someone who can dive deep into the potential of an idea and help me refine it, rather than just generating more ideas that might not fit.

That brings me to Watanabe-san. She's 57, also reflective, and a general member in our department. I think she'd be an excellent first choice. We share a similar work style, which means she'd likely appreciate the groundwork I'd have already done. I could present my most promising initial concept or two, outlining the core feeling, a rough story seed, and some potential content directions. She wouldn't just jump in with her own new ideas right away; instead, she'd probably ask insightful questions, poke holes constructively, and help me see angles I might have missed. Her reflective nature would complement mine, allowing us to build on each other's structured thinking. It's a low-pressure environment to flesh out the bones of an idea before bringing it to someone more intuitive or to the leader.

For instance, one idea that's been percolating, stepping away from traditional sports, is something that taps into the human spirit of overcoming challenges and building something beautiful, much like the dedication you see in athletes, but in a non-athletic context. I'm thinking of a game called something like "Echoes of the Skyforge."

**The Concept:** It's a life simulation and cooperative puzzle game with light RPG elements. Imagine a world where ancient, colossal mechanisms lie dormant, scattered across a breathtaking, sky-island archipelago. These are not tools of war, but relics designed to harmonize the environment, control weather, and nurture growth. Over centuries, they've fallen silent. Players are part of an intrepid guild of "Skyweavers," who use their ingenuity and teamwork to reactivate these forgotten behemoths.

**The Story:** The world is slowly losing its balance. Storms are growing harsher, crops are failing, and the very sky islands are beginning to crumble. A prophecy speaks of the Skyweavers, individuals chosen to rekindle the heart of the world. Each activated mechanism reveals a piece of the history of the old civilization and hints at an overarching mystery threatening the world's existence. The ultimate goal is to reactivate the "Grand Harmonizer," a central, continent-sized mechanism that will restore balance forever.

**Contents:**
*   **Exploration:** Players would fly or glide between beautiful sky islands, discovering ruins and dormant mechanisms. Each island would have its own unique biomes, flora, and fauna, and environmental puzzles.
*   **Mechanism Restoration:** This is the core gameplay. It's a mix of puzzle-solving (figuring out ancient circuits, replacing lost gears), resource management (gathering rare crystalline components, refining alloys), and cooperative building. Teams of players would have to work together to lift heavy parts, align complex energy conduits, and even defend their progress from environmental hazards or mischievous elemental spirits.
*   **Life Simulation Elements:** Between mechanism restoration missions, players return to their guild's floating hub. Here, they can craft tools, research lost technologies, cultivate unique sky-island crops for food and resources, and even raise specialized flying mounts for faster exploration. There would be light character progression, earning skills related to engineering, resource gathering, and piloting.
*   **Social & Cooperative Focus:** The game would emphasize working together. Some puzzles would explicitly require multiple players performing different actions simultaneously. There would be a strong sense of shared accomplishment and community as the world visibly heals and flourishes with each reactivated mechanism.
*   **Narrative Progression:** As mechanisms are restored, the world changes. New areas become accessible, weather patterns normalize, and ancient secrets are unveiled through discovered lore, journals, and even holographic projections from the past.

**Slogan:** "Ignite the past. Weave the future. Together."

**Key Selling Points:**
1.  **Unique Cooperative Puzzle-Adventure:** A refreshing blend of puzzle-solving, exploration, and cooperative gameplay without combat being the primary focus.
2.  **Visually Stunning World:** An emphasis on breathtaking sky-island vistas, intricate ancient machinery, and a vibrant, healing environment.
