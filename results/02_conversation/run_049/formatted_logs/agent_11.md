## ■ ターン 0: 初期状態
* **【初期思考】**
> Right, okay, new project brief. "Design story, concept, contents, slogan, key selling points" for a new game, totally free ideas, even outside our department's 'military/war' broad genre. Gameplay style can be anything. Think outside the box. This sounds pretty exciting, actually! No technical limits or anything like that to worry about just yet, just pure creative thinking. That's my kind of task.

Hmm, "military/war game planning" is our department, but they really emphasized that we can incorporate other elements or choose not to at all. They want us to think outside the box. That's good, because honestly, I'm not always keen on just pure, gritty war games. I mean, they're fine, but there's so much more you can do with the *themes* of military or conflict without making it a standard FPS or RTS.

My initial gut feeling is to twist the "military" aspect into something unexpected. What if it's not about fighting *in* a war, but about the elements surrounding it, or even preventing it, or dealing with the aftermath? Or what if it's about the more human, often overlooked, side of things?

Let's see... an intuitive jump here... what if we leaned into the *cooperative* and *life simulation* aspects, but with a military backdrop? Not a typical combat game. How about a game focused on an elite, specialized unit, but their missions aren't always about direct combat? Maybe they're focused on rescue, humanitarian aid, or even environmental preservation in conflict zones. Or, completely different, what if it's about the development of military technology, but through a puzzle or strategy lens that has ethical choices?

No, wait, I've got something that feels a bit more fresh. What if the "war" isn't against another nation, but against a global catastrophe, and the "military" is simply the best-organized global response force? Like, a natural disaster, or a rapidly spreading ecological threat.

Okay, my brain is sparking now. Here’s a rough idea: "Echoes of Tomorrow."
The concept is a *cooperative life simulation/strategy game* set in a near-future world, maybe 50-100 years from now. Humanity has faced a devastating, world-altering event – let's say "The Great Silence," where some unknown energy phenomenon rendered vast areas of the planet uninhabitable or mutated flora and fauna into dangerous, unpredictable forms. It's not a war between humans, but humanity vs. the consequences of this event.

The players are part of an elite, international "Reclamation Corps" – basically, a military-like organization, but their mission is to re-establish safe zones, rebuild infrastructure, and understand/neutralize the lingering effects of The Great Silence. It's about survival, exploration, and cooperation, not combat against other humans.

*   **Story:** Humanity is scattered in small, defensible settlements. Your squad is deployed to retrieve crucial ancient technologies, secure vital resources, or establish new, stable outposts in the "Silent Zones." Each mission unravels more about "The Great Silence" and hints at a way to reverse or mitigate its effects, or perhaps even discover what caused it. There could be personal stories of loss, hope, and resilience woven throughout, focusing on individual squad members and the communities they help.

*   **Concept:** "Strategic survival and reconstruction." Players manage a squad of specialists (e.g., Engineer, Scout, Medic, Biologist, Security). Each mission requires careful planning, resource management, and team synergy. Decisions made in the field affect not just the mission outcome, but also the morale and development of the home base. It’s less about shooting and more about problem-solving, crafting, exploration, and protecting vulnerable people or resources from mutated wildlife or environmental hazards. Think "XCOM" meets "This War of Mine" meets "Fallout" (but cooperative and less combat-focused).

*   **Contents:**
    *   **Character Progression:** Unlock new skills, equipment, and specializations for your squad members. Maybe each character has a backstory that affects their abilities or motivation.
    *   **Base Building/Management:** Between missions, manage and expand your main "Reclamation Hub," researching new technologies, crafting advanced gear, and training new recruits.
    *   **Procedurally Generated Zones:** Missions take place in varied environments (ruined cities, overgrown forests, desolate deserts, irradiated mountains) with dynamic threats and objectives.
    *   **Ethical Dilemmas:** Players face tough choices – save a group of survivors but risk mission failure, or prioritize the objective? Use a dangerous experimental technology for a breakthrough, or stick to safer, slower methods?
    *   **Cooperative Multiplayer:** Up to four players can control a specialist within the same squad, each contributing their unique skills. Communication and coordination are key.
    *   **Dynamic Weather/Environmental Hazards:** Sandstorms, acid rain, energy anomalies – these aren't just visual, they impact gameplay and require tactical responses.

*   **Slogan:** "Rebuild the World. Reclaim Humanity." Or maybe, "The War for Tomorrow Begins Today." I kinda like "Rebuild the World. Reclaim Humanity." It's more hopeful and less aggressive.
