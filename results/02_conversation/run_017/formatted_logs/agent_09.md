**Concept:** It's a cooperative, narrative-driven life simulation and resource management game set in a unique, post-war world, but not one of devastation. Instead, it's a world where the old 'war machines' and technology have been repurposed or left to grow wild, creating beautiful, alien landscapes. The "military" aspect isn't about fighting, but about organized survival, repurposing old tech, and collective memory.
**Story:** Players are part of a small, diverse community attempting to re-establish a thriving society generations after a silent, devastating "War of the Whispers" which wasn't fought with bombs, but with information warfare that subtly shifted reality. The remnants of that war are strange, dormant mechanical leviathans in the landscape, or echoes of old data streams that can be harnessed or corrupted.
**Contents:**
*   **Base Building/Community Management:** Players work together to build a settlement, allocate resources, and make collective decisions.
*   **Exploration:** Venture into the overgrown, surreal landscapes, discovering dormant war machines that can be salvaged for unique resources or contain lost histories. Each expedition is like a small puzzle or strategic encounter, not combat.
*   **Narrative Choices:** The "war" element comes from managing lingering societal divisions, external threats from mutated wildlife or rival scavenger groups (who also avoid direct combat, favoring resource contention or sabotage), and uncovering the true history of the "Whispers."
*   **Crafting/Repurposing:** Salvaged military tech isn't for weapons, but for advanced farming, communication devices, aesthetic elements, or even quirky transportation.
**Slogan:** "The greatest battles are fought not with swords, but with dreams." Or maybe, "Where peace is forged from forgotten wars."
**Key Selling Points:**
1.  **Unique Post-Conflict Setting:** A world transformed by subtle warfare, not explicit destruction, creating a visually stunning and thought-provoking environment.
2.  **Cooperative Strategy & Life Sim:** Blends the strategic depth of military planning (resource allocation, long-term goals, risk assessment) with the satisfying progression of a life simulation and strong cooperative play.
3.  **Narrative-Driven Exploration:** Unravel a rich, mysterious past through discovery and choices, where the 'enemy' is often misinformation or the environment itself.
4.  **Repurposing & Creativity:** Focus on salvaging and transforming old war tech into tools for peace and innovation, rather than destruction.
