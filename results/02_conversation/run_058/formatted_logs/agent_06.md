**How to approach this task?**

My initial approach will be to facilitate a dedicated brainstorming session with my entire team.

1.  **Kick-off Meeting:** I'll schedule a meeting, making it clear that it's a "blue sky" session. The goal is pure idea generation, no filtering initially. I'll emphasize that we're looking for creativity and that no idea is too outlandish at this stage.
2.  **Setting the Stage:** I'll reiterate the project context: "Sci-Fi is our department's broad strategic direction, but we can incorporate anything. Think action, RPG, puzzle, life sim – mix and match! We're designing the whole package: story, concept, content, slogan, key selling points."
3.  **Individual Prep (Optional but encouraged):** Before the main session, I might send out an email asking everyone to come up with at least one or two very rough, high-level game concepts on their own. This gives the reflective thinkers, like Watanabe-san, time to mull things over and avoids putting them on the spot in real-time. It also allows the intuitive types to jot down their initial bursts of inspiration.
4.  **Structured Brainstorming (but flexible):** During the meeting, I'll probably start by having everyone quickly share their initial ideas, perhaps with a time limit per person to keep things moving. We won't critique immediately, just get everything out there. I'll make sure to note down keywords, themes, and potential gameplay loops.
5.  **Grouping and Expanding:** After the initial dump, we can look for common threads or really intriguing outlier ideas. We can then start grouping similar concepts or pick a few promising ones to expand upon as a group. "What if we took X's story idea and combined it with Y's gameplay mechanic?"
6.  **Focus on the "Why":** As we develop ideas, I'll encourage the team to think about the *why*. Why would players care about this story? What makes this gameplay engaging? What's the core emotional hook? This will naturally lead us toward the key selling points.
7.  **Slogan & Title:** Once we have a stronger concept, we can brainstorm slogans and potential working titles. These often emerge organically from the core concept.

My work style is "Balanced," so I'll try to ensure a good mix of free-flowing creativity and some gentle guidance to keep us on track. I'll make sure everyone gets a chance to speak and their ideas are heard. I'm excited to see what my team comes up with! This is where the real fun of game development starts.
