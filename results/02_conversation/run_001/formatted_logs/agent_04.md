Okay, so, the concept: **"Everyday Heroism in a Super-Powered World."**

The **story** could be about a relatively ordinary person – let's call her Rika, a young barista or maybe a librarian – living in Neo-Kyoto, a vibrant city constantly being saved (and sometimes inadvertently damaged) by a revolving cast of flamboyant, often self-absorbed, 'S-Class' heroes. The **concept** is a "Life-Simulation RPG" with strong narrative choices and puzzle elements. It's not about punching villains; it's about patching up the world the heroes forget about, or are too busy to notice.

**Contents** would include:
*   **Daily Life Simulation:** Managing Rika's job, relationships, apartment, and personal goals.
*   **Emergency Response Mini-Games:** When hero battles occur, you're thrust into time-sensitive scenarios. This could involve puzzle-solving (e.g., how to safely clear a path through rubble), quick-time events (e.g., pulling someone out of harm's way), or resource management (e.g., distributing first aid kits, finding lost pets).
*   **Reputation System:** Your actions build your reputation not with the S-Class heroes, but with the ordinary citizens and local community leaders. High reputation unlocks new opportunities, resources, and even changes how certain NPCs react to you.
*   **Branching Narrative:** Decisions Rika makes about who to help, what risks to take, or even what to say to a distraught citizen can lead to different story arcs and outcomes. Maybe she uncovers a conspiracy that even the S-Class heroes miss because they're too focused on flashy combat.
*   **"Small-Scale Impact" System:** See the tangible results of your actions. A park you helped clean up becomes a community gathering spot. A person you helped now volunteers alongside you.
*   **"Hero Interactions":** Occasionally, Rika might cross paths with the S-Class heroes, but usually in awkward, comedic, or slightly frustrating ways. They might accidentally destroy her new cafe, or mistake her for a villain's accomplice. These interactions could be dialogue-driven or small, light-hearted challenges.
