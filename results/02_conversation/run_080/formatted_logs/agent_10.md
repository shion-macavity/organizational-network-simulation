## ■ ターン 0: 初期状態
* **【初期思考】**
> Okay, deep breath. This is exciting! A chance to really just… *think*. "Fantasy Game Planning," but with complete freedom for genre and style, and I can mix anything. "Think outside the box" – that's a dream come true for me. As Yoshida, I definitely want to take some time to really chew on this before I start bouncing ideas off anyone. My reflective work style means I prefer to get my own thoughts organized first.

So, what kind of game idea could I pitch? Something fantasy, but not *just* fantasy. I want to subvert expectations a little, or combine things in a fresh way. The goal is to design the story, concept, contents, slogan, and key selling points.

Let's start with a core concept. What if magic isn't some grand, flashy thing just for heroes, but something that exists in the mundane, in the everyday? What if the focus isn't on slaying dragons, but on understanding the quiet, subtle magic that shapes the world and people's lives?

Okay, picture this: A world where magic isn't a spell you cast to shoot fireballs, but something more... atmospheric, intrinsic. It's tied to emotions, to nature, to forgotten stories. It influences people in subtle ways, almost like a pervasive emotional current or a hidden natural force. And what if someone's job was to understand and *manage* this?

So, my idea: **"Echoes of the Heart: The Weavers' Almanac."**

**Story:**
The world is called Aerthos. It's a land where emotions and memories have a faint, lingering magical resonance – "echoes." These echoes accumulate in specific places, on objects, or within certain individuals, sometimes creating beautiful, benevolent phenomena, and other times manifesting as disruptive, confusing, or even melancholic events. A forgotten argument might manifest as a persistent chill in a room, or a joyous reunion might cause wildflowers to bloom out of season. Most people live their lives oblivious to the source, simply experiencing these "quirks."
The player is a newly initiated "Echo Weaver," a member of a secretive order dedicated to understanding, cataloging, and gently guiding these emotional resonances. They don't cast flashy spells, but use ancient tools, intuition, and empathy to perceive, untangle, and sometimes re-weave these echoes, bringing balance back to places and people.
The main narrative arc could involve a growing "cacophony" of unresolved echoes threatening to overwhelm Aerthos, leading to widespread emotional disarray and strange phenomena. The player discovers this isn't random; an ancient, powerful echo is stirring, one connected to a forgotten tragedy or a pivotal moment in history, and it's amplifying all other echoes. They must trace this primary echo back to its source, understand its story, and find a way to bring it peace, thus harmonizing the world.

**Concept:**
It's a **Narrative-driven Life Simulation RPG with Puzzle and Exploration elements.** The core concept is about emotional archaeology and empathetic problem-solving. It's less about combat and more about observation, deduction, and interaction. Players learn to "read" the emotional landscape of Aerthos, collecting fragments of stories and feelings, and gently influencing them for the better. It's a game about connection, healing, and understanding the invisible threads that tie us all together.

**Contents/Gameplay:**

1.  **Echo Perception & Collection:** The player character possesses a unique sensitivity. Through a UI element that visually represents emotional "gradients" or "pulses" in the environment, they can perceive echoes. They use tools like "Resonance Charms" (to amplify weak echoes), "Memory Lenses" (to glimpse past events), and "Empathy Staves" (to soothe or gently redirect chaotic echoes). This involves mini-games or observation-based puzzles to "attune" to an echo and collect its story fragments.
2.  **The Weaver's Almanac:** A comprehensive in-game journal where players catalog collected echoes, their origins, effects, and successful resolutions. This acts as a progression system and a lore repository.
3.  **Community & Relationships:** As the player helps resolve echoes in different towns and villages, they build relationships with the inhabitants. These relationships can unlock new areas, tools, or offer unique perspectives on certain echoes. Some echoes might even be tied directly to a specific NPC's emotional history. This is where the "life simulation" aspect comes in – daily interactions, small favors, building trust.
4.  **Environmental Storytelling:** Every location tells a story through its echoes. A bustling market might have echoes of joy and minor frustrations, while an abandoned ruin might hold powerful echoes of despair or forgotten glory. The environment itself becomes a character.
5.  **Puzzle-Solving:** Echoes manifest as puzzles. For instance, a "Tangled Echo" might require players to trace emotional threads through an environment to find the core conflict, then use their tools to untangle and re-harmonize them in the correct sequence or combination. A "Lingering Echo" might require piecing together fragmented memories to understand a past event before it can be resolved. There are no "wrong" answers in a destructive sense, but there are more harmonious and less harmonious resolutions.
6.  **Customization:** Player character customization, and perhaps customizing their small "Sanctuary" or base of operations where they can reflect on their findings and craft new tools.

**Slogan:**
"Listen to the world's heart. Mend its forgotten melodies."
