So, who to consult first and how to approach this...

My first thought is always to get the team together. I've got Nakamura, Kobayashi, Kato, Yoshida, Yamada, and Sasaki. Most of them are more on the reflective side, which is excellent. My intuitive approach often means I jump to a concept quickly, and they'll be good at finding the gaps, refining, and making sure it's solid. But for kicking things off, I want pure, unadulterated creativity.

So, my strategy would be to call for an initial brainstorming session with my entire department. I wouldn't go in with a fully formed idea, or even a partially formed one. I'd set the stage: "Folks, this isn't about fitting a mold. It's about breaking it. Forget everything you think a 'sports game' has to be. What kind of game do *you* genuinely want to play? What's an experience that would truly grab players?" I'd emphasize that there are no bad ideas at this stage, no silly concepts. We're looking for that spark. I'd probably even throw out a few outlandish hypotheticals myself just to loosen everyone up and show them how far out we can go. Something like, "What if we had a cooking competition... but it's an extreme sport in an alien kitchen?" just to get them thinking laterally.

Once we've got a pile of wild ideas, then we can start to see if any of them naturally lean towards something we can develop. My intuitive side will help me spot a strong core concept or a unique hook from the disparate suggestions. I'll be looking for something that just *feels* right, something that resonates emotionally, something with a strong, immediate appeal.

Specifically, I'd want to nudge them towards ideas that blend familiar sports elements with something completely unexpected. For example, instead of just a soccer game, what if it's "Zero-G Galactic Soccer," where teams manipulate gravitational fields to score goals, and each player has unique psychic abilities? Or what about a "Mind-Sport Olympics," where the competition isn't physical, but about mental fortitude, strategy, and even dream manipulation? We could have "Dream Curling" or "Psionic Marathon."

Let's say we lean into something like "Mind-Sport Olympics."
*   **Concept:** A futuristic world where humanity has evolved beyond physical sports, embracing 'Mind-Sports' – competitions of intellect, strategy, and mental prowess, often visualized as elaborate, fantastical arenas within the collective consciousness.
*   **Story:** Players are up-and-coming 'Psion Athletes' entering the annual Mind-Sport Olympics. They must train their mental faculties, overcome personal demons (represented as in-game challenges), and uncover a conspiracy threatening to corrupt the pure spirit of the Mind-Sports. Each 'sport' could be a distinct genre mini-game.
*   **Contents:**
    *   **"Thought Track":** A racing game where you navigate complex, ever-changing mental landscapes, dodging distractions and maintaining focus. (Think F-Zero meets a puzzle game).
    *   **"Emotion Chess":** A strategic board game where pieces represent emotional states, and their moves influence opponents' psychological stability. (Think Chess meets a psychological horror element, but competitive).
    *   **"Dream Weave":** A cooperative puzzle game where players link up mentally to build intricate dreamscapes, solving challenges by creating shared illusions. (Think Portal meets a creative sandbox).
    *   **"Memory Dive":** A mystery/exploration game where players delve into the fragmented memories of other Psion Athletes to uncover secrets or weaknesses.
    *   **Customization:** Psion Athlete avatars, mental 'gear' (visual effects for their powers), and personal dream-space customization.
    *   **Multiplayer:** Competitive leaderboards for Thought Track and Emotion Chess, cooperative challenges for Dream Weave.
*   **Slogan:** "Compete Beyond Limits. Play Beyond Reality." Or maybe, "The Field Is Your Mind."
*   **Key Selling Points:**
    1.  **Revolutionary Concept:** Merges the thrill of sports with deep psychological and fantastical elements.
    2.  **Diverse Gameplay:** A unique collection of genre-bending mini-games under one compelling narrative.
    3.  **Unleash Your Mind:** Engage in strategic, puzzle-solving, and creative competitions that challenge intellect, not just reflexes.
    4.  **Rich Lore:** Immerse yourself in a vibrant, futuristic world of Psion Athletes and mental mastery.

I think once we have a solid concept like this, I'll probably want to touch base with Yamamoto from the Superhero Game Planning department. She's also intuitive. It might be good to bounce ideas off someone outside my immediate team, get a fresh, quick take. Sometimes another intuitive person can instantly see the potential or spot a major flaw without needing a long explanation. Plus, the idea of "psychic abilities" or "superhuman mental feats" might even resonate with her department's focus, and perhaps we could find some cross-pollination of ideas, or just get an objective, high-level gut check. She might even have ideas on how to make the 'hero' journey aspect of the story more engaging. But that's a second step. First, I want to ignite the creative fire right here in my own department.
