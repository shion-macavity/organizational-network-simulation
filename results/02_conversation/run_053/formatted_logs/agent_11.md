## ■ ターン 0: 初期状態
* **【初期思考】**
> Alright, new project, new game idea! This is where the fun begins, completely free ideas. "Slice of Life" is our department's genre, but they're saying we can pretty much do anything. Action, RPG, puzzle, mystery... everything's on the table. This is perfect for someone like me, I like to just let the ideas flow and see where they go.

Okay, so first things first, I need to come up with *something*. A core concept, a story hook, then I can build out the contents, slogan, and selling points. Thinking outside the box is key here.

Slice of life... what does that truly mean when you can blend anything? It’s about everyday experiences, emotions, relationships, growth. What if we twist that? What if the "life" isn't strictly human, or the "slice" is from something much larger than expected?

Hmm, here's an idea that just popped into my head... A game where you play as... well, not a human, but a *spirit* or a *guardian* of a very specific, mundane location. Let's call it "Echoes of the Ordinary."

**Concept:** You are the ancient, unseen guardian spirit of an old, beloved local library. Not a grand, magical library, but a community hub, a bit worn around the edges, filled with the everyday lives of the people who visit. Your job isn't to fight monsters or go on epic quests, but to subtly influence events, connect people, mend relationships, and generally foster a sense of warmth and discovery within the library's walls. It's a slice of life, but from a non-human, ethereal perspective.

**Story:** The library is facing closure due to budget cuts and dwindling visitors. As the guardian spirit, awakened by the distress of the few loyal patrons, you must subtly guide the community to rediscover the library's value. You experience snippets of their lives – a lonely elderly man seeking companionship in books, a stressed student struggling with an essay, a shy child looking for adventure, a young couple having their first date in the quiet corners. Your "actions" manifest as small, seemingly coincidental events: a forgotten book "mysteriously" reappearing on the right shelf, a quiet corner becoming unusually inviting, a helpful note appearing in a textbook, two strangers "accidentally" bumping into each other and striking up a conversation. Through these small, interconnected events, you help the library thrive and save itself.

**Contents (Gameplay):**
*   **Observation & Empathy System:** Players "listen" to the thoughts and feelings of patrons by focusing on them. This reveals their needs, problems, and desires.
*   **Subtle Influence Mechanics:** Instead of direct actions, you have a limited pool of "spiritual energy" to trigger various "nudges." For example:
    *   *Book Resonance:* Make a specific book subtly glow or fall from a shelf, drawing a patron's attention to it.
    *   *Whisper of Thought:* Plant a gentle idea in someone's mind, like "Maybe I should talk to them," or "This book seems interesting."
    *   *Ambient Shift:* Alter the atmosphere of a section – make a study area feel more focused, a reading nook more comforting.
    *   *Path Weaving:* Gently encourage two people to cross paths, potentially leading to an interaction.
*   **Time Progression & Seasons:** The game unfolds over a year or two, reflecting the slow pace of community change. Seasonal events like a summer reading program or a winter charity drive would offer unique challenges and opportunities.
*   **Relationship Web:** As you influence interactions, you'd see a visual representation of how characters' relationships evolve – friendships blooming, rivalries softening, families reconnecting.
*   **Mini-narratives:** Each patron has their own evolving story, and you contribute to their personal arcs. Success isn't about "winning" but about fostering happiness and connection.

**Slogan:** "The quiet magic in every story. Your library, reimagined." Or maybe something a bit more evocative like, "Where every book has a soul, and every soul finds its story." No, I think the first one is stronger, "The quiet magic in every story. Your library, reimagined." Yeah, that feels right.

**Key Selling Points:**
1.  **Unique Perspective:** Play as an unseen guardian spirit, influencing human lives indirectly.
2.  **Emotional Depth:** Focus on genuine human connection, empathy, and the quiet beauty of everyday life.
3.  **Low-Stress, High-Impact Gameplay:** A relaxing yet deeply rewarding experience where small actions have meaningful consequences.
4.  **Community Building:** Experience the satisfaction of fostering a vibrant, supportive community.
5.  **Reimagined "Slice of Life":** Takes the core genre and twists it into something fresh and innovative.

Yamaguchi, our leader, is also intuitive. He'd probably appreciate the outside-the-box thinking, but he's also the one who has to summarize it. I want to make sure the core idea is appealing beyond just the initial "wow" factor.
