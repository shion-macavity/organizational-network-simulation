Okay, here's a rough initial concept, just for my own thoughts:

**Concept:** "Echoes of Eden" (working title)
It's a cooperative, life simulation, and resource management game set in a post-apocalyptic world, but not one ravaged by typical human-on-human warfare. Instead, it's after a series of devastating, accelerated climate events – massive floods, superstorms, prolonged droughts – have rendered much of the planet uninhabitable. Humanity is pushed to the brink, forced into scattered, isolated settlements. The "military" aspect isn't about combat with other humans, but about the *strategic defense and rebuilding* of these last bastions of civilization against the relentless, unpredictable forces of nature and dwindling resources.

**Story:** Players lead a small, diverse community of survivors, having escaped a final, cataclysmic event. They find themselves in a relatively stable but fragile sanctuary, perhaps a floating city built on reclaimed oceanic debris, or a vast underground complex, or even a community thriving within a gigantic, mutated forest. The goal isn't just to survive day-to-day, but to rediscover lost knowledge, reverse the environmental degradation in their immediate vicinity, and eventually, reconnect with other rumored survivor enclaves to pool resources and plan for humanity's long-term future. It's a story of resilience, hope, and the human spirit's capacity to adapt and overcome.

**Contents:**
*   **Base Building & Management:** Players manage their settlement's infrastructure – power, food, water purification, waste management, housing, research facilities. Resources are scarce and often volatile.
*   **Exploration & Scavenging:** Players send out specialized teams into the dangerous, ever-changing ruined world to scavenge for rare materials, lost technology, and plant seeds. These expeditions are strategic puzzles, requiring careful planning of routes, supplies, and risk assessment against environmental hazards (e.g., acid rain, sudden radiation storms, predatory mutated wildlife).
*   **Research & Development:** A deep tech tree focused on ecological restoration, advanced survival techniques, and sustainable energy. Unlocking things like terraforming modules, advanced atmospheric purifiers, and genetic modification of crops.
*   **Dynamic Environmental Threats:** The world itself is the primary antagonist. Players constantly face environmental challenges like resource depletion, disease outbreaks exacerbated by the climate, and unpredictable weather patterns that can destroy infrastructure or block routes.
*   **Cooperative Multiplayer:** Multiple player communities can exist in the same world instance. They can trade resources, send aid missions, or even merge to form larger, more resilient societies. Communication and diplomacy with other player-led settlements would be crucial.
