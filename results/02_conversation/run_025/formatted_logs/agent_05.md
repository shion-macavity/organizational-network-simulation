**Concept:** Players are "Star Weavers," cosmic architects tasked with preserving fragments of dying civilizations across the universe. The "sport" is a competitive race against time and other Star Weavers to reconstruct these lost worlds within vast, procedurally generated celestial canvases. It's not about combat, but about discovery, interpretation, and creation.

**Story:** In the distant future, the universe is slowly cooling, and countless civilizations are fading into oblivion. A celestial organization, the "Cosmic Archivists," has developed a technology that allows highly skilled individuals, Star Weavers, to enter "echo-zones"—temporal and spatial distortions containing the last remnants of these dying worlds. Their mission is to piece together the stories, cultures, and even entire ecosystems of these civilizations before their echoes completely vanish. However, resources are scarce, and rival Star Weaver factions compete to gather the most valuable "echoes" and reconstruct the most complete and beautiful "Chronicles of Light" to earn favor and the right to influence the next era of cosmic seeding.

**Contents (Gameplay):**
*   **Exploration:** Players would navigate sprawling, ethereal landscapes – a mix of asteroid fields, nebulae, and ghost-like cities. Think open-world exploration, but in a non-Euclidean space where physics can be bent.
*   **Puzzle/Discovery:** Within these echo-zones, players find "memory fragments" – visual, auditory, and tactile clues that piece together the history of a lost world. These could be like environmental puzzles, where you need to activate dormant technology, reconstruct ancient structures, or even interpret abstract art.
*   **Creation/Reconstruction:** Once enough fragments are gathered, players can begin to "reweave" the world. This involves using collected resources to generate flora, fauna, cities, and even historical events in their own chosen cosmic canvas. This is where the artistic and strategic elements come in. Do you recreate it exactly as it was, or interpret it in a new, more vibrant way?
*   **Competitive Element:** Other Star Weavers are also in the echo-zones, collecting fragments. You might encounter them, and while direct combat isn't the focus, there could be strategic interactions:
    *   **Resource Depletion:** Fragments are finite, so you need to be quick.
    *   **"Truth" Sabotage:** Planting false fragments or misleading trails to throw off rivals.
    *   **Showcasing:** At certain intervals, all players' "Chronicles of Light" are judged by cosmic entities for accuracy, completeness, aesthetic beauty, and emotional impact. This is the "scoring" part of the sport.
*   **Life Simulation/Progression:** Players would have their own Star Weaver vessel or base, which they can upgrade, customize, and use to process fragments. There could be skill trees for faster exploration, better reconstruction, or more effective "truth" manipulation.

**Slogan:** "Weave the Past. Command the Future. Become the Echo."

**Key Selling Points:**
1.  **Unique Genre Blend:** Combines vast open-world exploration, intricate puzzle-solving, creative sandbox elements, and strategic competition in a truly innovative way.
2.  **Unparalleled Visuals:** Imagine cosmic ballet meeting ancient ruins, all dynamically rendered and influenced by player actions.
3.  **Meaningful Player Choice:** Players don't just follow a story; they reconstruct and interpret it, leading to highly personalized game experiences.
4.  **Infinite Replayability:** Procedurally generated echo-zones and diverse civilization types ensure every playthrough offers new mysteries and challenges.
5.  **Skill-Based Competition:** Success hinges on keen observation, creative problem-solving, strategic thinking, and a touch of artistic flair, appealing to a wide range of competitive players.
