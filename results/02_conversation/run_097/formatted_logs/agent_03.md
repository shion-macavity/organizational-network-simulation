**The Core Concept:** You play as a personal assistant, handler, or even a close friend/family member to someone who has just discovered they have superpowers. It's not about you having powers, but about you *managing* the hero's life, both secret and public.

**Story:** Our protagonist, a young, earnest individual, gets a new job as a personal assistant, only to discover their eccentric boss is actually a fledgling superhero, "Captain Catalyst," who unintentionally received powers during a bizarre lab accident. Captain Catalyst is clumsy, well-meaning, and totally overwhelmed. The story revolves around the player helping Captain Catalyst balance their normal life (paying bills, going to therapy for their power-induced anxiety, learning to control their abilities) with their burgeoning heroic duties (stopping petty criminals, dealing with collateral damage, learning to be a public figure). There are slice-of-life moments, moral dilemmas, and eventually, a larger threat that emerges as Captain Catalyst gains more notoriety.

**Contents/Gameplay:**
*   **Daily Management:** A time-management element where you schedule Captain Catalyst's day: training sessions (mini-games for power control), public appearances, "normal person" appointments, and crime-fighting missions.
*   **Problem Solving:** Dialogue choices and mini-puzzles to help Captain Catalyst navigate social situations, ethical dilemmas (e.g., "Do we expose a corrupt politician, or focus on a bank robber?"), and personal issues.
*   **Resource Management:** You'd manage their public image, their dwindling savings (because heroics don't pay!), and their mental health. Maybe you have to choose between buying a new "hero suit" material or paying the rent.
*   **Relationship Building:** Develop relationships with other characters – a cynical police detective, a helpful scientist, a nosy reporter, Captain Catalyst's worried family. Your choices impact these relationships and unlock different story paths.
*   **"No-Combat" Heroics:** The focus isn't on direct combat. Instead, it's about strategizing, gathering information, and using Captain Catalyst's specific, often awkward, powers creatively to resolve situations without excessive destruction or violence. Maybe Captain Catalyst's power is to turn solid objects into goo, so you have to figure out *how* to use goo to stop a robbery without harming anyone.

**Slogan:** "Behind every hero, there's a legend." Or maybe, "The real work of saving the world starts *before* the spotlight."

**Key Selling Points:**
1.  **Unique Perspective:** Experience the superhero genre from an entirely fresh, human perspective – the unsung hero managing the chaos.
2.  **Narrative Depth:** A rich story driven by player choices, moral dilemmas, and the relatable struggles of balancing extraordinary powers with ordinary life.
3.  **Engaging Gameplay Loop:** A compelling mix of time management, puzzle-solving, and relationship building, keeping players constantly engaged in their role.
4.  **Heart and Humor:** A blend of poignant moments and lighthearted humor, showing the charm and absurdity of superhero life from the ground up.
5.  **Focus on Strategy, Not Combat:** Appeal to players who love strategic thinking and narrative-heavy games rather than pure action, offering a thoughtful take on heroism.
