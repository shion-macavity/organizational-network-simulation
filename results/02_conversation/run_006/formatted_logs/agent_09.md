## ■ ターン 0: 初期状態
* **【初期思考】**
> Alright, let's get this brain-dump started. A new game project, huh? This is exciting! "Free ideas," they said, "think outside the box." That's exactly how I like to work. No rigid framework, just pure imagination. My department is Sports Game Planning, but they said that's just a broad direction, we can go anywhere with it. That's a green light for some real creativity!

So, story, concept, content, slogan, key selling points... Phew, a lot to chew on. My mind just races when I hear "free ideas." I'm not really one for sitting down and analyzing every angle first; I just want to get the core idea out there.

What really captures attention? Something unique, something that brings people together but also lets them show off their skills. Traditional sports games can be great, but we've seen a lot of them, haven't we? What if we took the *spirit* of sports – competition, skill, strategy, teamwork – and put it into a completely different kind of setting?

I'm thinking... what if we created a game where players design and manage a team of unique "creatures" or "champions," not for direct combat like an RPG, but for elaborate, dynamic "races" or "trials" across fantastical landscapes? Imagine something like an obstacle course, but every "obstacle" is a puzzle, a strategic decision, or requires a specific skill from your chosen champions.

Let's call it... "Chrono-Relay: Epoch Gauntlet."
The **story** could be something about ancient civilizations leaving behind these incredible, time-bending gauntlets or trials, and players are part of a guild or a new generation of "Epoch Runners" who discover them. Their goal is to master these trials, uncover the secrets of the past, and ultimately compete in the Grand Epoch Gauntlet to restore balance or achieve some legendary status. There could be a subtle mystery element about the creators of these gauntlets, maybe even some light lore to uncover about the creatures themselves.

The **concept** is a blend of competitive racing, strategic team building, and puzzle-solving, all wrapped in a visually stunning, fantastical setting. Players would collect and train different "Chrono-Beasts" or "Epoch-Champs," each with unique abilities – some can fly, some can burrow, some have super strength, some can manipulate time on a small scale, or even interact with environmental elements in specific ways. Before each "Gauntlet Run," you'd strategically assemble a team of three to five champions, knowing the challenges ahead.

**Contents** would include a vast array of unique Gauntlet courses, each with different themes and environmental hazards – ancient ruins, cosmic jungles, floating ice islands, volcanic caverns. You'd have a roster of dozens of different Chrono-Beasts to collect, level up, and customize visually. There would be a deep training system, maybe even a 'bonding' system with your creatures that unlocks new team abilities. Gameplay modes could include single-player story progression, daily/weekly challenges, and, of course, competitive multiplayer where players race their teams against each other either directly or in ghost-data challenges. Co-op could be interesting too, where two players manage parts of a larger team for an even more complex Gauntlet.

For a **slogan**, something punchy... how about: "Master Time. Master the Gauntlet. Master Your Legend." Or maybe: "Beyond the Finish Line, Your Saga Awaits." The second one feels a bit more epic. Let's lean into that. "Beyond the Finish Line, Your Saga Awaits."

And the **key selling points**?
1.  **Unleash Your Ultimate Team:** Collect, train, and customize a diverse roster of fantastical Chrono-Beasts, each with unique abilities.
2.  **Strategic Racing & Puzzle Mastery:** Conquer dynamic, evolving gauntlets that demand both quick reflexes and smart team composition.
3.  **A World of Wonder & Mystery:** Immerse yourself in breathtaking, time-bending landscapes filled with ancient secrets to uncover.
4.  **Compete & Collaborate:** Challenge rivals globally or team up with friends in a variety of thrilling multiplayer modes.

Yeah, I think that's a solid foundation. It's got elements of sports competition, but it breaks free from the mold. It's got RPG elements with the creature collection and leveling, action and racing for gameplay, and a bit of mystery for the story.

He's also intuitive and probably has a lot of experience. He's 64, so he's seen a lot, and might have some wild ideas to add or a perspective I haven't considered. He probably wouldn't shut down a "crazy" idea right away, which is what I need at this stage.

Sasaki-san (ID:12) is also intuitive, and 54, so another experienced perspective that might vibe with this kind of thinking. Maybe I should talk to both of them, informally, just to see what sparks.

I wouldn't go straight to Yamaguchi-san (ID:13), our department leader, with just a raw idea like this. I want to flesh it out a bit more first, get some validation or some more angles to strengthen it. He's reflective, so he'd probably want to see a bit more structure and justification than I have right now. Nakamura-san (ID:7) is also reflective, and a lot younger, so she might appreciate a more polished pitch too. I'd definitely want her input later for how a younger audience might react, but for initial brainstorming, I prefer to throw spaghetti at the wall with someone who enjoys throwing spaghetti too.
