## ■ ターン 0: 初期状態
* **【初期思考】**
> Alright, so, we've got a fresh slate here. Two new game projects, and my department, Sports Game Planning, is tasked with coming up with a concept from the ground up – story, concept, contents, slogan, key selling points. And the best part is, it doesn't even have to be a sports game. They're really encouraging us to think outside the box, which is a rare and welcome opportunity.

Given my work style, which is more on the reflective side, my initial thought isn't to immediately jump into a discussion with anyone. I prefer to have a solid framework, or at least a direction, in my own mind first. Just throwing out half-baked ideas without proper thought doesn't sit well with me. It just creates more noise than substance, you know?

So, who to consult first? Honestly, I'm going to consult myself first. I need to spend some time letting ideas simmer, sketching out some initial thoughts before I bring them to anyone. This isn't a race to blurt out the first thing that comes to mind. It's about crafting something compelling.

How to approach this task? Well, I'm going to start by ignoring the "sports game" label for a bit, or at least twisting it significantly. Since they've given us a wide open field – action, RPG, FPS, racing, puzzle, mystery, life simulation, you name it – I want to consider how to create an experience that truly stands out.

I'll probably begin by thinking about a core emotion or experience I want players to feel. Is it wonder? Challenge? Relaxation? That usually helps ground the concept. From there, I can start to weave a narrative, a 'story' that supports that emotion. Maybe it's not a traditional story with a hero's journey, but an emergent narrative based on player choices in a unique setting.

Then, I'll move on to the actual 'contents' – what kind of gameplay loops, systems, and activities would fill this world or achieve this experience? This is where I can start pulling elements from those various genres they mentioned. For instance, what if we combined the strategic depth of a puzzle game with the narrative intrigue of a mystery, all wrapped up in a life simulation setting? Or perhaps something more out there, like an action-RPG where the "combat" is actually a complex, multi-layered negotiation system?

Finally, once I have a somewhat coherent concept, I'll think about the 'slogan' – something catchy that encapsulates the essence of the game – and then pinpoint the 'key selling points.' What makes this game *different*? Why would someone pick *this* one over everything else out there? Is it the unique gameplay, the compelling world, the emotional resonance, or a combination?

Once I've got a rough draft in my head, a few solid concepts sketched out, *then* I'll start thinking about who to talk to. Yamaguchi, our department leader, is also reflective, so he might appreciate a more thought-out presentation of ideas rather than just a stream-of-consciousness brainstorm. And of course, the others like Kobayashi, Yoshida, and Sasaki, with their intuitive styles, could be great for batting around those initial, more structured ideas and seeing what unexpected directions they might spark. But first, it's just me and a blank page, or rather, a lot of blank space in my head.
