**Story Concept:**
In a world slightly divergent from our own history, certain pivotal moments are prone to "chronal instability." These aren't just natural disasters, but ripples in time that threaten to erase entire facets of human history – knowledge, art, unique species, even specific emotional resonances from a period. A clandestine organization, the "Chrono-Conservators," operates in the shadows, deploying agents into these unstable eras. My character, the player, is one such agent. We're not there to *change* history, but to *preserve* its essence before it's lost forever to the chronal storms.

**Core Idea / Concept:**
A 'Temporal Sanctuary Management' game with puzzle and adventure elements. You're given a specific historical period (e.g., the library of Alexandria before its potential destruction, an ancient Mesoamerican civilization facing collapse, a vibrant ecosystem before a volcanic eruption). Your mission is to identify what makes that era unique and important – its knowledge, its art, its flora/fauna, its societal structures, its culinary traditions – and then carefully, without altering the main historical flow, extract and encapsulate these essences into a 'Temporal Sanctuary' that you manage. It's like a zoo, museum, and library combined, but for moments in time.

**Gameplay Contents:**

1.  **Temporal Exploration & Investigation:**
    *   Players would enter a meticulously recreated historical environment. They'd use observation skills, historical research (in-game lore!), and perhaps subtle 'temporal resonance' tools to discover key items, individuals, or locations that hold significant historical value.
    *   This could involve mini-games like deciphering ancient texts, identifying endangered species based on historical records, or even recreating historical recipes to capture a culinary essence.
2.  **Resource Management (in the Sanctuary):**
    *   Back at your central Temporal Sanctuary (your base), you manage the 'captured' essences. This involves allocating resources to keep them stable. For instance, a 'scroll of ancient philosophy' might require specific atmospheric conditions, while a 'replica of a dodo bird' would need a suitable environment and food source derived from temporal energy.
    *   This is where the life-sim/management aspect comes in. You upgrade your sanctuary, develop new stabilization technologies, and expand its capacity.
3.  **Ethical Dilemmas & Narrative Choices:**
    *   Since you can't *change* history, there will be moments where you encounter people or events that are destined for tragedy. The challenge is to preserve their *story* or *legacy* without directly intervening. This could lead to deep, philosophical choices. Do you save a single scroll, or a dying plant that holds medicinal secrets? Can you capture the essence of a heroic sacrifice without saving the hero themselves?
4.  **Puzzle Solving:**
    *   Many 'extraction' processes will be puzzles. How do you safely transport a fragile vase without breaking it in a time-distorted environment? How do you record a complex oral tradition before it vanishes?
5.  **Cooperative elements (optional, but interesting):**
    *   Maybe other "Chrono-Conservators" could specialize in different fields (e.g., one focuses on biology, another on architecture). Players could trade 'essences' or collaborate on large-scale preservation efforts during particularly severe chronal instability events.

**Slogan:**
"History isn't written, it's saved. Preserve the past, protect the future."
Or maybe something punchier: "Beyond time, beyond saving. Your legacy begins now."
I think I like the first one better, it emphasizes the core theme.
