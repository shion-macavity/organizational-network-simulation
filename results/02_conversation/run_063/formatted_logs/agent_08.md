* **【初期思考】**
> Alright, let's get this brain simmering. New game idea, huh? And we can really go wild with it, which is exciting! So, 'fantasy'... but not necessarily swords and dragons, unless we give it a wild twist. How about we flip the usual fantasy trope? Instead of being a mighty hero destined to save the world, what if we're a very ordinary person in a world *filled* with fading magic, and our job is to nurture it back to life in small, meaningful ways?

I'm thinking something that's not about grand battles, but about quiet discovery, creativity, and connection. Maybe a blend of a life sim, a crafting game, and a gentle puzzle game.

Here's a rough, super-early concept popping into my head:

**Concept:** "Whispering Blooms" (or something similar). It's a cozy, enchanting life simulation game set in a subtly magical, post-post-apocalyptic world. Magic isn't gone, it's just dormant, hidden in plain sight, and slowly fading from human memory. The player is a "Dream Weaver" or a "Spirit Gardener"—someone who feels the whispers of this forgotten magic.

**Story:** You've inherited a small, neglected plot of land, maybe an old overgrown cottage in a valley. As you begin to tend to the land, clear the weeds, and repair the old structures, you start uncovering relics of a forgotten era where magic was interwoven with daily life. These aren't powerful spells, but little sparks—a fountain that once grew crystalline flowers, a loom that wove starlight into cloth, ancient runes that hum when specific flora grows nearby. Your goal is to restore these "magical gardens" or "spirit sanctuaries" by cultivating unique magical plants, crafting enchanted items, and solving environmental puzzles that awaken the land's inherent magic. As you bring more magic back, the world around you slowly transforms – colors become more vibrant, unique creatures might appear, and even the weather patterns might subtly shift. You can connect with other villagers, some of whom remember fragmented stories of the old magic, and work together to rediscover ancient secrets.

**Contents:**
*   **Gardening & Cultivation:** Discover and grow hundreds of unique magical plants, each with specific requirements (moonlight, starlight, different soil types, specific elemental energies). These aren't just decorative; they are key to activating magical points.
*   **Crafting & Enchantment:** Use harvested magical flora, minerals, and other discoveries to craft enchanted tools, furniture, and unique items that aid in your restoration efforts or bring beauty to your home.
*   **Exploration & Puzzle Solving:** Explore ancient ruins, hidden groves, and forgotten libraries, piecing together clues to understand the land's magical history. Many "puzzles" would be environmental, like arranging specific plants to redirect magic flow, or creating a certain environment to attract a rare spirit.
*   **Spirit Companions:** As magic blooms, gentle nature spirits or small magical creatures might be drawn to your restored areas. They could offer small buffs or help with tasks.
*   **Social Simulation:** Interact with a small cast of charming villagers, each with their own stories and knowledge about the fading magic. Build relationships, trade goods, and perhaps even collaborate on larger restoration projects.
*   **Home & Land Customization:** Fully customize your cottage and expand your land, turning it into a thriving magical haven.

**Slogan:** "Tend the Earth. Awaken the Magic. Weave Your Own Wonder."
Or, maybe simpler: "Whispers of Magic. A Gentle Bloom."

**Key Selling Points:**
1.  **Unique, Relaxing Fantasy Experience:** Move beyond combat-heavy RPGs into a serene world of discovery and creation. Perfect for players seeking a calming, yet deeply engaging game.
2.  **Rich & Deep Gardening/Crafting System:** Hundreds of unique magical plants and recipes with intricate interdependencies and subtle magical effects.
3.  **Heartfelt Story of Restoration:** A narrative that focuses on healing, rediscovering beauty, and the quiet power of nurturing, rather than epic battles.
4.  **Beautiful, Evolving World:** Watch your efforts visibly transform the environment, bringing vibrant life and subtle magic back to the world around you.

Okay, that's a pretty good start for a free-form idea. It's got that 'fantasy' core but definitely not what you'd typically expect, which fits the "outside the box" brief. I think this could appeal to a really wide audience, especially those looking for something calming and creative.

Now, who should I talk to first about this? My immediate thought is to bounce it off someone who also has an intuitive workstyle. Nakamura-san and Yamada-san come to mind. They might get the 'vibe' of it quickly and help me expand on the big picture before we try to iron out too many details. Yamaguchi-san is our leader, so I'll want to get this a bit more fleshed out before presenting it to her. Kato-san is balanced, so she might be good for a second round of feedback, perhaps helping to ground some of the wilder ideas.

So, yeah, I'll probably try to catch Nakamura-san or Yamada-san first, just to see what they think and if they have any initial sparks to add. This is just a draft, after all.
